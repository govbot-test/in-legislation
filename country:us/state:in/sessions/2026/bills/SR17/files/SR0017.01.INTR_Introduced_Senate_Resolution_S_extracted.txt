SENATE RESOLUTION ____
MR. PRESIDENT:
I offer the following resolution and move its adoption:
1 A SENATE RESOLUTION memorializing Richard D.
2 Young.
3 Whereas, State Senator Richard D. Young, Jr., of Milltown,
4 Indiana, beloved husband, father, grandfather, and great-
5 grandfather, passed away on Saturday December 27, 2025, at
6 his home;
7 Whereas, Senator Young was born on December 2, 1942,
8 in Louisville, Kentucky, to Richard D. Young, Sr., and
9 Margaret Haywood;
10 Whereas, Senator Young graduated from St. Xavier High
11 School in Louisville, Kentucky, in 1961 and graduated summa
12 cum laude from Vincennes University with an A.B. in
13 Behavioral Sciences;
14 Whereas, Senator Young was married to his first wife,
15 Elaine, for nearly 41 years until her passing;
16 Whereas, Senator Young married his second wife, Ashira,
17 in 2005 and she survives him;

2
1 Whereas, Senator Young represented District 47, which
2 included parts of Crawford, Dubois, Harrison, Orange, and
3 Washington Counties, after succeeding newly-elected
4 Lieutenant Governor Frank O'Bannon in 1988;
5 Whereas, Senator Young became the Senate's longest
6 serving Minority Leader after holding that position from 1996
7 to 2008 and later served as Minority Leader Emeritus from
8 2008 to 2014;
9 Whereas, In 2009, Senator Young co-founded the Rural
10 Caucus, which continues to be a bi-partisan group of
11 legislators in the General Assembly focused on rural
12 communities and agricultural issues;
13 Whereas, Senator Young served as the Ranking Minority
14 Member on the Agriculture and Natural Resources and Local
15 Government committees;
16 Whereas, Prior to his tenure in the Senate, Senator Young
17 was the Crawford County Auditor for eight years and also
18 served as the Secretary of the Crawford County Democrat
19 Central Committee from 1986 to 1988;
20 Whereas, Prior to entering politics, Senator Young was
21 part-owner of E&S Distributors in Louisville, Kentucky, with
22 his two brothers, Phillip and Tom. After moving to Milltown,
23 Indiana, in 1970, he raised cattle and hogs on his 120-acre
24 farm until his entrance into politics;
25 Whereas, After his marriage to his second wife, Ashira,
26 Senator Young raised goats and chickens and had a large
27 vegetable garden. He also dedicated his time to oil painting and
28 making jewelry. It could be said that Senator Young was an
29 unassuming Renaissance man;
30 Whereas, Senator Young always provided his Senate
31 colleagues and staff with eggs from his farm;

1 Whereas, Senator Young is survived by four daughters,
