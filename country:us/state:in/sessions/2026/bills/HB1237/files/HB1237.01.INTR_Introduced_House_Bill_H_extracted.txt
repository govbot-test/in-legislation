DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 4-6-15-4.
Synopsis: Use of opioid settlement funds. Provides that distributions
to the local abatement opioid settlement account to cities, counties, and
towns may be used for the procurement of mobile radio equipment by
a city, county, or town for use by first responders, in addition to
programs of treatment, prevention, and care that are best practices as
defined or required by a settlement document or court order under
current law.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Genda
January 5, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Local Government.

A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning state
offices and administration.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 4-6-15-4, AS AMENDED BY P.L.201-2023,
2 SECTION 58, IS AMENDED TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE
3 JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 4. (a) Except as provided by any bankruptcy court
4 order or bankruptcy settlement, and subject to subsection (g), funds
5 received from opioid litigation settlements that resolve existing state
6 and political subdivision litigation lawsuits as of January 1, 2021, shall
7 be distributed in a minimum of two (2) payments per year in the
8 following manner:
9 (1) Fifteen percent (15%) to the state unrestricted opioid
10 settlement account established by IC 4-12-16.2-5(1) for the
11 benefit of the state.
12 (2) Fifteen percent (15%) to the local unrestricted opioid
13 settlement account established by IC 4-12-16.3-5(1) for
14 distribution as reimbursement to cities, counties, and towns
15 according to a weighted distribution formula identified in
16 settlement documents that accounts for opioid impacts in
17 communities.

1 (3) Thirty-five percent (35%) to the state abatement opioid
2 settlement account established by IC 4-12-16.2-5(2) to be used for
3 statewide treatment, education, and prevention programs for
4 opioid use disorder and any co-occurring substance use disorder
5 or mental health issues as defined or required by the settlement
6 documents or court order.
7 (4) Thirty-five percent (35%) to the local abatement opioid
8 settlement account established by IC 4-12-16.3-5(2) for
9 distribution to cities, counties, and towns according to a weighted
10 distribution formula identified in settlement documents that
11 accounts for opioid impacts in communities. However, if a city's
12 or town's annual distribution under this subdivision is:
13 (A) for a distribution made before July 1, 2023, less than one
14 thousand dollars ($1,000); or
15 (B) for a distribution made after June 30, 2023, less than five
16 thousand dollars ($5,000);
17 the city's or town's annual distribution must instead be distributed
18 to the county in which the city or town is located.
19 (5) Distributions under this subdivision (4) may be used only for:
20 (A) programs of treatment, prevention, and care that are best
21 practices as defined or required by the settlement documents
22 or court order; or
23 (B) the procurement of mobile radio equipment by a city,
24 county, or town for use by first responders, as designated
25 under IC 10-10.5-2-1.
26 (b) Any attorney's fees or costs required to be paid by the state,
27 including any amount in a settlement designated for payment of state
28 attorney's fees or costs, shall be deducted from the distribution
29 described in subsection (a)(1), even if the funds have not been
30 deposited in the agency settlement fund.
31 (c) The amounts distributed under subsection (a)(2) and (a)(4) are
32 annually appropriated to the office of the attorney general to make the
33 distributions described under subsection (a)(2) and (a)(4).
34 (d) Funds received from the settlement may not be distributed to a
35 city, county, or town that has opted out of the settlement under section
36 2(b) of this chapter. The settlement funds that are not distributed to the
37 cities, counties, or towns that have opted out of the settlement must be
38 distributed in the manner set forth under subsection (a)(2) and (a)(4)
39 to the cities, counties, or towns that have opted into the settlement.
40 (e) All entities receiving opioid settlement funds shall monitor the
41 use of those funds and provide an annual report to the office of the
42 secretary of family and social services not later than a date determined

1 by the office of the secretary of family and social services.
2 (f) The office of the secretary of family and social services shall
3 compile and submit an annual comprehensive report of the information
4 received under subsection (e) to the general assembly in an electronic
5 format under IC 5-14-6 not later than October 1 of each year
6 identifying all funds committed and used as specified by any settlement
7 documents or court order.
8 (g) If any settlement documents or court order, assurance of
9 voluntary compliance, or other form of agreement related to opioids
10 requires at least seventy percent (70%) of the settlement proceeds to be
11 used for treatment, education, recovery, or prevention programs, any
12 amount of settlement funds in addition to those distributed under
13 subsection (a)(3) and (a)(4) that are needed to meet the terms must first
14 come from funds that would otherwise be distributed under subsection
15 (a)(1).
16 (h) Any city, county, or town receiving a distribution under
17 subsection (a)(2) or (a)(4) may transfer all or part of its distribution to
18 another city, county, or town to be used for the benefit of both
19 communities.
20 (i) Upon a majority vote of the legislative body, a city, county, or
21 town receiving a distribution under subsection (a)(2) or (a)(4) may sell
22 for cash or other consideration the right to receive the distribution.
23 However, the proceeds from the sale of a distribution received under
24 subsection (a)(2) must be used for the purposes allowed for a
25 distribution under subsection (a)(2), and the proceeds from the sale of
26 a distribution received under subsection (a)(4) must be used for the
27 purposes allowed for a distribution under subsection (a)(4). A city,
28 county, or town may pledge, grant a lien on, or grant a security interest
29 in a distribution to effectuate a sale under this subsection. The
30 legislative body's approval of the sale is conclusive as to the adequacy
31 of the consideration for the sale.
