1 A HOUSE RESOLUTION recognizing March 17, 2026,
2 as Profound Autism Day and April as National Autism Month.
3 Whereas, Autism is a lifelong neurological condition
4 encompassing a wide range of lived experiences, and growing
5 awareness has shown that some autistic individuals require
6 minimal support while others require significant, around-the-
7 clock care;
8 Whereas, The term profound autism has emerged to
9 provide clarity regarding autistic individuals with particularly
10 intensive support needs, including those who may require
11 continuous assistance from an adult caregiver and who may
12 experience significant intellectual, communication, or adaptive
13 challenges;

1 Whereas, Individuals with profound autism are frequently
2 underrepresented or excluded from research initiatives, service
3 systems, and public policy discussions due to the complexity
4 and intensity of their needs;
5 Whereas, In April 2023, the Centers for Disease Control
6 and Prevention released its first national prevalence study on
7 profound autism, finding that approximately one in four
8 8-year-old children with autism in the United States meet the
9 criteria for profound autism;
10 Whereas, The study also found that children with profound
11 autism are more likely to come from economically
12 disadvantaged backgrounds and, compared with children with
13 non-profound autism, often face greater medical,
14 developmental, and social challenges that increase support
15 needs and contribute to isolation for both individuals and their
16 families;
17 Whereas, Individuals with profound autism and those who
18 care for them are part of our communities, and the families,
19 caregivers, educators, and professionals who support them play
20 an essential role that deserves recognition and support; and
21 Whereas, Meaningful inclusion of individuals with
22 profound autism requires deliberate acknowledgment of their
23 unique challenges and a commitment to ensuring that public
24 awareness, research efforts, and support systems reflect the full
25 diversity of the autism spectrum: Therefore,
26 Be it resolved by the House of Representatives of the
27 General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
28 SECTION 1. That the Indiana House of Representatives
29 recognizes March 17, 2026, as Profound Autism Day, and April
30 as National Autism Month, to honor individuals with autism
31 across the spectrum, including those with profound autism, and
32 to encourage greater understanding, inclusion, and support for
33 them and for those who provide lifelong care.
