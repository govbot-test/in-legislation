Synopsis: Aggravated speeding. Provides for a penalty enhancement
for aggravated speeding committed in the commission of certain

HOUSE BILL No. 1211
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning
criminal law and procedure.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 35-50-2-20 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA CODE
2 AS A NEW SECTION TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE JULY
3 1, 2026]: Sec. 20. (a) As used in this section, "aggravated speeding"
4 means operating a motor vehicle in excess of:
5 (1) one hundred (100) miles per hour; or
6 (2) thirty (30) miles per hour greater than the maximum
7 posted speed limit.
8 (b) As used in this section, "offense" means a felony described
9 in IC 35-42-2-2(b)(1)(B) or IC 35-42-2-2(b)(2)(B).
10 (c) The state may seek, on a page separate from the rest of the
11 charging instrument, to have a person who allegedly committed an
12 offense sentenced to an additional fixed term of imprisonment if
13 the state can show beyond a reasonable doubt that the person
14 knowingly or intentionally committed aggravated speeding in the
15 commission of the offense.
16 (d) If the person is convicted of the offense in a jury trial, the
17 jury shall reconvene to hear evidence in the enhancement hearing.
2026 IN 1211—LS 6453/DI 151
2
1 If the trial was to the court, or the judgment was entered on a
2 guilty plea, the court alone shall hear evidence in the enhancement
3 hearing.
4 (e) If the jury (if the hearing is by jury) or the court (if the
5 hearing is to the court alone) finds that the state has proven beyond
6 a reasonable doubt that the person knowingly or intentionally
7 committed aggravated speeding in the commission of the offense,
8 the court may sentence the person to an additional fixed term of
9 imprisonment of not more than five (5) years.
10 (f) A sentence imposed under this section shall run consecutively
11 to the underlying sentence.
