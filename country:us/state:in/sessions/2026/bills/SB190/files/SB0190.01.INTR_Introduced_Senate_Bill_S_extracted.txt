DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 35-31.5-2; IC 35-43-4-10.
Synopsis: Crimes concerning gift cards. Establishes the offenses of
gift card theft, a Class A misdemeanor, and gift card fraud, a Level 6
felony.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Carrasco, Walker K
January 6, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Corrections and Criminal
Law.

SENATE BILL No. 190
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning
criminal law and procedure.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 35-31.5-2-67.7 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA
2 CODE AS A NEW SECTION TO READ AS FOLLOWS
3 [EFFECTIVE JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 67.7. "Counterfeit gift card", for
4 purposes of IC 35-43-4-10, has the meaning set forth in
5 IC 35-43-4-10(a).
6 SECTION 2. IC 35-31.5-2-143.6 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA
7 CODE AS A NEW SECTION TO READ AS FOLLOWS
8 [EFFECTIVE JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 143.6. "Gift card", for purposes
9 of IC 35-43-4-10, has the meaning set forth in IC 35-43-4-10(a).
10 SECTION 3. IC 35-31.5-2-143.8 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA
11 CODE AS A NEW SECTION TO READ AS FOLLOWS
12 [EFFECTIVE JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 143.8. "Gift card redemption
13 information", for purposes of IC 35-43-4-10, has the meaning set
14 forth in IC 35-43-4-10(a).
15 SECTION 4. IC 35-43-4-10 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA CODE
16 AS A NEW SECTION TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE JULY
17 1, 2026]: Sec. 10. (a) The following definitions apply throughout
2026 IN 190—LS 6923/DI 149
2
1 this section:
2 (1) "Counterfeit gift card" means:
3 (A) an object that is not a gift card but contains identifying
4 information that purports to be a gift card; or
5 (B) a gift card that has been tampered with or altered.
6 (2) "Gift card" means a card, code, or other object that is:
7 (A) activated or inactive;
8 (B) issued on a prepaid basis in a specified amount; and
9 (C) redeemable for specified goods or services.
10 (3) "Gift card redemption information" means:
11 (A) the digital data contained within a gift card's magnetic
12 strip or chip; or
13 (B) information that is unique to a gift card that allows a
14 person to access, transfer, or spend the funds that are on
15 the gift card.
16 (b) A person who knowingly or intentionally exerts
17 unauthorized control over:
18 (1) a gift card; or
19 (2) gift card redemption information;
20 commits gift card theft, a Class A misdemeanor. However, the
21 offense is a Level 6 felony if the person has a prior unrelated
22 conviction under this subsection.
23 (c) A person who knowingly or intentionally:
24 (1) creates or possesses a counterfeit gift card;
25 (2) places a counterfeit gift card in a retail location where a
26 consumer would customarily purchase a gift card; or
27 (3) tampers with a gift card or gift card redemption
28 information;
29 commits gift card fraud, a Level 6 felony. However, the offense is
30 a Level 5 felony if the person has a prior unrelated conviction
31 under this subsection.
