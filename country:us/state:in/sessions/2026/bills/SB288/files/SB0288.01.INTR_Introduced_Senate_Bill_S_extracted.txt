SENATE BILL No. 288
_____
DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 22-2-21.2.
Synopsis: Organ donation. Provides that an employee who has worked
for an employer for at least a year is entitled to take an unpaid leave of
absence for organ donation. Provides that a leave of absence may not
exceed 30 working days per calendar year. Requires an eligible
employee to provide notice to the employee's employer before taking
a leave of absence. Provides that an employer may require an eligible
employee to take other leave before using the leave of absence.
Effective: July 1, 2026.

4 Chapter 21.2. Leave for Organ Donation
5 Sec. 1. As used in this chapter, "employee" means a person
6 employed or permitted to work or perform services for
7 remuneration under a contract of hire, written or oral, by an
8 employer.
9 Sec. 2. As used in this chapter, "employer" means the following:
10 (1) A sole proprietor, corporation, partnership, limited
11 liability company, or other entity with one (1) or more
12 employees.
13 (2) A state agency.
14 (3) A political subdivision (as defined in IC 36-1-2-13).
15 Sec. 3. As used in this chapter, "state agency" means an
16 authority, board, branch, commission, committee, department,
17 division, or other instrumentality of any of the following:

1 (1) The executive, including the administrative department of
2 state government.
3 (2) The legislative department of state government.
4 (3) The judicial department of state government.
5 (4) A state educational institution.
6 (5) A body corporate and politic created by statute.
7 Sec. 4. (a) An employee who has been employed by an employer
8 for at least twelve (12) months is entitled to an unpaid leave of
9 absence for organ donation, including recovery and testing, as
10 provided in this chapter.
11 (b) A leave of absence under this chapter may not exceed a total
12 of thirty (30) working days per calendar year.
13 (c) An eligible employee may elect, or an employer may require
14 the employee, to substitute any earned paid leave, except medical
15 or sick leave, for the leave provided under this chapter.
16 Sec. 5. An eligible employee who wants to take a leave of
17 absence under this chapter shall provide written notice to the
18 employee's employer of the date the leave will begin. An employee
19 shall give at least thirty (30) days notice before the date on which
20 the employee intends to begin the leave.
21 Sec. 6. Nothing in this chapter restricts an employer from
22 providing:
23 (1) more than thirty (30) working days of leave per calendar
24 year; or
25 (2) paid leave.
26 Sec. 7. An employer may not retaliate against an employee for
27 requesting or obtaining a leave of absence under this chapter.
28 Sec. 8. If an employee is eligible to take a leave of absence under
29 this chapter and IC 4-15-16, the employee may only take leave
30 under IC 4-15-16.
