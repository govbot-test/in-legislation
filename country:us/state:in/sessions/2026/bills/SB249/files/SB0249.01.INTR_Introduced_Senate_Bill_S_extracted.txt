SENATE BILL No. 249
_____
DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 15-19-7.
Synopsis: State chemist. Codifies animal food administrative rules.
Defines or amends certain terms. Codifies certain requirements and
processes for commercial feed and pet food with respect to: (1) label
format; (2) label information; (3) express guarantees; (4) suitability; (5)
ingredients; (6) directions for use and precautionary statements; (7)
nonprotein nitrogen; (8) drug and feed additives; (9) adulterants; (10)
good manufacturing practices; (11) payment of inspection fees; (12)
interstate exclusion; and (13) commercial feed licenses.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Leising
January 8, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Agriculture.

7 [EFFECTIVE JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 25.9. (a) Except as required in

11 (1) The statement must be separate and distinct from the

14 (2) The statement must be measured in terms of metabolizable

16 kilocalories per kilogram (kcal/kg) of product, and may also
17 be expressed as kilocalories per familiar household measure,

19 (3) The calorie content is determined by one (1) of the
20 following methods:
21 (A) By calculation using the following modified Atwater
22 formula:
23 ME(kcal/kg) = 10[(3.5 × CP) + (8.5 × CF) +
24 (3.5 × NFE)]
25 Where: ME = Metabolizable energy.
26 CP = % crude protein as-fed.
27 CF = % crude fat as-fed.
28 NFE = % nitrogen-free extract (carbohydrate) as-fed.
29 The percentages of CP and CF are the arithmetic averages
30 from proximate analyses of at least four (4) production
31 batches of the product, and the NFE is calculated as the
32 difference between one hundred (100) and the sum of CP,
33 CF, and the percentages of crude fiber, moisture, and ash
34 (determined in the same manner as CP and CF).
35 (B) In accordance with a testing procedure established by
36 Association of American Feed Control Officials.
37 (4) An affidavit must be provided upon request to the state
