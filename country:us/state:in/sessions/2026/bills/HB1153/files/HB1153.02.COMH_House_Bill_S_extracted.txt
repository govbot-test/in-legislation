DIGEST OF HB 1153 (Updated January 27, 2026 11:46 am - DI 137)
Citations Affected: IC 9-32.
Synopsis: Auto dealer matters. Requires a dealer to collect and retain
certain identifying information from a potential purchaser. Beginning
July 1, 2026, requires a transport operator to either: (1) have an
established place of business with a physical Indiana address; or (2)
provide the secretary of state (secretary) with proof of the transport
operator's valid registration from the United States Department of
Transportation authorizing the transport operator to operate within
Indiana; to apply for a registration number. Provides that a dealership
that operates without a license from the secretary commits a Class A
infraction.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Lawson, Pressel, Heine, Harris
January 5, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Roads and Transportation.
January 27, 2026, amended, reported — Do Pass.

HOUSE BILL No. 1153
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning
motor vehicles.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 9-32-3-14 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA CODE
2 AS A NEW SECTION TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE JULY
3 1, 2026]: Sec. 14. A dealer must obtain and retain one (1) of the
4 following from a potential purchaser:
5 (1) From an individual, a photocopy or electronic scan of the
6 individual's driver's license or identification card.
7 (2) From a business, one (1) of the following:
8 (A) The business's federal employer identification number.
9 (B) A photocopy or electronic scan of a driver's license,
10 identification card, or other government issued
11 photographic identification card of an owner, officer, or
12 other individual authorized to act on behalf of the business,
13 together with the name of the business.
14 SECTION 2. IC 9-32-7.5-2, AS AMENDED BY P.L.182-2021,
15 SECTION 11, IS AMENDED TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE
16 JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 2. (a) This section does not apply to a vehicle
17 registered as a recovery vehicle under IC 9-18.1-6.
HB 1153—LS 6705/DI 137
2
1 (b) A transport operator may, instead of registering each motor
2 vehicle transported or disposable trailer used, make a verified
3 application upon a form prescribed by the secretary and furnished by
4 the secretary for a general distinctive registration number for:
5 (1) all motor vehicles transported by the transport operator and
6 used and operated for the purposes provided; or
7 (2) all disposable trailers used and operated for the purpose of
8 transporting sectionalized buildings.
9 (c) The application must contain the following:
10 (1) A brief description of:
11 (A) each style or type of motor vehicle transported or the type
12 of disposable trailer used to transport the sectionalized
13 building, whichever is applicable; and
14 (B) the manner in which the transport operator intends to use
15 the plates.
16 (2) The name and address of the transport operator.
17 (3) For an application to use a disposable trailer, a statement that
18 the disposable trailer will be disassembled after a single use.
19 (4) Any other information the secretary requires.
20 (d) The secretary may not issue transport operator license plates to
21 a transport operator that has been convicted of violating this article
22 until the secretary is satisfied that the transport operator is able to
23 comply with the requirements of this section.
24 (e) Beginning July 1, 2026, a transport operator must either:
25 (1) have an established place of business with a physical
26 Indiana address; or
27 (2) if the transport operator does not have an established
28 place of business in Indiana, provide the secretary with proof
29 of the transport operator's valid registration from the United
30 States Department of Transportation authorizing the
31 transport operator to transport within Indiana;
32 to make a verified application under this section.
33 SECTION 3. IC 9-32-16-17 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA CODE
34 AS A NEW SECTION TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE JULY
35 1, 2026]: Sec. 17. (a) A dealership must be licensed by the secretary
36 of state under this article to operate in Indiana.
37 (b) A dealership that violates this subsection commits a Class A
38 infraction.
