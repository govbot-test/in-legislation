Citations Affected: IC 15-20-5.5.
Synopsis: Prohibition on declawing cats. Prohibits a person from
declawing cats in Indiana.
Effective: July 1, 2026.

Development.

HOUSE BILL No. 1220
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning
agriculture and animals.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 15-20-5.5 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA CODE
2 AS A NEW CHAPTER TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE
3 JULY 1, 2026]:
4 Chapter 5.5. Cat Declawing
5 Sec. 1. As used in this chapter, "cat" means an animal of the
6 species Felis catus.
7 Sec. 2. As used in this chapter, "declawing" means an
8 onychectomy or any other surgical procedure to:
9 (1) amputate; or
10 (2) modify;
11 a portion of a cat's paw in order to remove the cat's claws.
12 Sec. 3. As used in this chapter, "tendonectomy" means a
13 procedure in which the tendons to a cat's:
14 (1) limbs;
15 (2) paws; or
16 (3) toes;
17 are cut or modified so that the normal functioning of the claws is
2026 IN 1220—LS 6298/DI 150
2
1 impaired.
2 Sec. 4. (a) As used in this chapter, "therapeutic purpose" means
3 a medically necessary procedure to address an existing or
4 recurring infection, disease, injury, or abnormal condition in a
5 cat's:
6 (1) claws;
7 (2) nail bed; or
8 (3) toe bone;
9 that jeopardizes the cat's health.
10 (b) The term does not include a procedure performed for a
11 cosmetic or aesthetic purpose or to make the cat more convenient
12 to keep or handle.
13 Sec. 5. (a) A person shall not perform:
14 (1) surgical claw removal;
15 (2) declawing; or
16 (3) a tendonectomy;
17 on a cat or otherwise alter a cat's toes, claws, or paws to prevent or
18 impair the normal function of the cat's toes, claws, or paws.
19 (b) This section does not apply to a procedure performed solely
20 for a therapeutic purpose.
21 (c) A person who violates this section is subject to a civil penalty
22 of:
23 (1) five hundred dollars ($500) for the first violation;
24 (2) one thousand dollars ($1,000) for the second violation; and
25 (3) two thousand five hundred dollars ($2,500) for the third
26 and any subsequent violation.
27 (d) Civil penalties collected under this section must be deposited
28 in the state general fund.
