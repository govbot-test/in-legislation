SENATE BILL No. 75
_____
DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 22-12-11.
Synopsis: Drive-through safety lanes. Requires certain drive-through
facilities constructed or renovated after December 31, 2026, to have a
separate bypass lane adjacent to the specified drive-through lanes.
Makes certain exceptions. Requires the fire prevention and building
safety commission to adopt administrative rules.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Ford J.D.
December 8, 2025, read first time and referred to Committee on Homeland Security and
Transportation.

4 Chapter 11. Drive-through Facility Safety
5 Sec. 1. As used in this chapter, a "drive-through facility" means
6 a drive-through financial institution, drive-through pharmacy, or
7 drive-through restaurant.
8 Sec. 2. As used in this chapter, "drive-through financial
9 institution" means a financial institution that has one (1) or more
10 driving lanes on the exterior of its permanent building site that
11 allow a customer to conduct financial transactions from inside the
12 customer's motor vehicle rather than conducting those
13 transactions inside the building.
14 Sec. 3. As used in this chapter, "drive-through pharmacy"
15 means a pharmacy that has one (1) or more driving lanes on the
16 exterior of its permanent building site that allow a customer to
17 drop off or pick up prescriptions while remaining inside the

22 (c) The owner of a drive-through facility shall ensure that any
23 drive-through lane serving the facility's customers has an adjacent
24 bypass lane that allows for the following:
25 (1) The operator of a motor vehicle in the drive-through lane
26 to drive the motor vehicle out of the drive-through lane and
27 into the bypass lane in order to exit the property.
28 (2) The operator of a public safety vehicle to drive alongside
29 or close to any motor vehicle in the drive-through lane in
30 order to respond to an emergency related to an operator or
31 passenger of the motor vehicle.
32 (d) A bypass lane described in subsection (c) shall meet the
33 following minimum standards:
34 (1) Be not less than ten (10) feet wide without obstructions.
35 (2) Contain adequate vertical and horizontal clearance for
36 standard passenger vehicles and public safety vehicles.
37 (3) Include clear, visible signage to alert vehicle operators to
38 the presence and direction of the bypass lane.
39 (4) Be designed so as to not obstruct pedestrian pathways, fire
40 lanes, public safety vehicle access to the permanent building,
41 or accessible parking spaces or routes for persons with
42 disabilities.
