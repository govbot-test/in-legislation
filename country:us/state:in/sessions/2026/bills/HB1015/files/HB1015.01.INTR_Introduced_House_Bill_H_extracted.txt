DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 10-16-7-26.
Synopsis: Indiana National Guard. Provides that the governor may
not, with certain exceptions, order a member of the Indiana National
Guard to perform any law enforcement duty that may result in the
surveillance, apprehension, detention, or arrest of an individual.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Gore
December 1, 2025, read first time and referred to Committee on Veterans Affairs and
Public Safety.
2026 IN 1015—LS 6266/DI 116
Introduced
Second Regular Session of the 124th General Assembly (2026)
PRINTING CODE. Amendments: Whenever an existing statute (or a section of the Indiana
Constitution) is being amended, the text of the existing provision will appear in this style type,
additions will appear in this style type, and deletions will appear in this style type.
Additions: Whenever a new statutory provision is being enacted (or a new constitutional
provision adopted), the text of the new provision will appear in this style type. Also, the
word NEW will appear in that style type in the introductory clause of each SECTION that adds
a new provision to the Indiana Code or the Indiana Constitution.
Conflict reconciliation: Text in a statute in this style type or this style type reconciles conflicts
between statutes enacted by the 2025 Regular Session of the General Assembly.
HOUSE BILL No. 1015
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning
public safety.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 10-16-7-26 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA CODE
2 AS A NEW SECTION TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE JULY
3 1, 2026]: Sec. 26. (a) This section does not apply to the following:
4 (1) An Indiana National Guard unit that has been federalized
5 under 10 U.S.C. 12301 or other applicable federal law.
6 (2) A member of the Indiana National Guard called to state
7 active duty in accordance with an emergency declared by the
8 governor under IC 10-14-3-12 for:
9 (A) a natural disaster;
10 (B) a riot; or
11 (C) an act of terrorism.
12 (b) This section may not be suspended under IC 10-14-3 or any
13 other law.
14 (c) Notwithstanding any other law, a governor may not order a
15 member of the Indiana National Guard to perform any law
16 enforcement duty that may result in the surveillance,
17 apprehension, detention, or arrest of an individual.
