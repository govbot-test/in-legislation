HOUSE BILL No. 1416

DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 34-13-3-3; IC 36-1.
Synopsis: Preemption of local regulation. Provides that unless
expressly authorized by another statute, a municipality or county may
not adopt, enforce, or maintain an ordinance, order, or rule regulating
conduct in a field of regulation that is occupied by a provision in
specified titles of the Indiana Code. Establishes a cause of action for a
person who has sustained an injury in fact, actual or threatened, from
a municipal or county ordinance, order, or rule adopted or enforced by
a municipality or county.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Miller D
January 8, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Judiciary.

15 Chapter 3.3. Regulatory Consistency Preemption of Local

17 Sec. 1. This chapter applies to a:

28 (5) IC 24 (Trade Regulation);
29 (6) IC 25 (Professions and Occupations);

31 (8) IC 28 (Financial Institutions); or
32 (9) IC 32 (Property).
33 An ordinance, order, or rule adopted by a county or municipality
34 that violates this section is void, unenforceable, and inconsistent
35 with this chapter.
