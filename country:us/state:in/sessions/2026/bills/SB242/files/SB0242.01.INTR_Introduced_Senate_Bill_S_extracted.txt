SENATE BILL No. 242
_____
DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 6-9; IC 35-52-6.
Synopsis: Innkeeper's tax. Authorizes the city of New Haven and the
city of Carmel to impose an innkeeper's tax at a flat rate that does not
exceed $5 per night. Makes corresponding changes to statutes
concerning innkeeper's tax administration.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Holdman
January 8, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Tax and Fiscal Policy.

26 Chapter 56.5. Carmel Innkeeper's Tax
27 Sec. 1. This chapter applies to the city of Carmel.
28 Sec. 2. The following definitions apply throughout this chapter:
29 (1) "Fiscal body" has the meaning set forth in IC 36-1-2-6.
30 (2) "Person" has the meaning set forth in IC 6-2.5-1-3.
31 Sec. 3. (a) Subject to section 7 of this chapter, the fiscal body of
32 the city may levy a tax on every person engaged in the business of
33 renting or furnishing, for periods of less than thirty (30) days, any
34 room or rooms, lodgings, or accommodations in any:
35 (1) hotel;
36 (2) motel;
37 (3) boat motel;
38 (4) inn;
39 (5) college or university memorial union;
40 (6) college or university residence hall or dormitory; or
41 (7) tourist cabin;
42 located in the city.

1 (b) The tax does not apply to a transaction in which:
2 (1) a student rents lodgings in a college or university residence
3 hall while that student participates in a course of study for
4 which the student receives college credit from a college or
5 university located in the city; or
6 (2) a person rents a room, lodging, or accommodations for a
7 period of thirty (30) days or more.

9 may not exceed the rate of five dollars ($5) per night. The tax is in
10 addition to:
11 (1) the state gross retail tax imposed under IC 6-2.5; and
12 (2) a county innkeeper's tax imposed in the county in which
13 the city is located, if any.
14 (d) The tax shall be imposed, paid, and collected in exactly the
15 same manner as the state gross retail tax is imposed, paid, and
16 collected under IC 6-2.5.
17 (e) All of the provisions of IC 6-2.5 relating to rights, duties,
18 liabilities, procedures, penalties, definitions, exemptions, and
19 administration are applicable to the imposition and administration
20 of the tax imposed under this section except to the extent those
21 provisions are in conflict or inconsistent with the specific
22 provisions of this chapter. The return to be filed for the payment
23 of the tax under this section may be either a separate return or
24 may be combined with the return filed for the payment of the state
25 gross retail tax as the department of state revenue may, by rule,
26 determine.
27 (f) The amounts received from the tax imposed under this
28 section shall be paid monthly by the treasurer of state to the fiscal
29 officer of the city upon warrants issued by the state comptroller.

34 chapter.

38 (1) To promote and encourage conventions, visitors, and
39 tourism within the city, including expenditures for
