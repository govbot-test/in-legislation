Introduced Version

DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: Noncode.
Synopsis: Rape kit processing. Requires the state police department
(department) to use part of an appropriation from House Enrolled Act
1001 (2025) to process rape kits and to eliminate the backlog of
untested rape kits. Permits the department to issue grants to law
enforcement agencies and testing labs to eliminate the backlog of
untested rape kits. Requires the department to make a report to the
general assembly by October 1, 2027, concerning the status of untested
rape kits.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Cash, Sweet
December 5, 2025, read first time and referred to Committee on Courts and Criminal Code.

HOUSE BILL No. 1073
A BILL FOR AN ACT concerning public safety.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. [EFFECTIVE JULY 1, 2026] (a) Of the eighteen
2 million dollars ($18,000,000) appropriated to the state police
3 department for forensic and health sciences laboratories in
4 SECTION 4(B) of P.L.213-2025 (HEA 1001-2025) for the state
5 fiscal year beginning July 1, 2026, and ending June 30, 2027, the
6 state police department shall use two million five hundred
7 thousand dollars ($2,500,000) to process rape kits and to eliminate
8 the backlog of untested rape kits.
9 (b) Funds disbursed under subsection (a) may be used to:
10 (1) purchase equipment, including robots, used to process
11 rape kits;
12 (2) process rape kits;
13 (3) hire staff;
14 (4) provide competitive wages for employees; and
15 (5) pay costs associated with streamlining the processing of
16 rape kits;
17 to eliminate the backlog of untested rape kits.
18 (c) The state police department may make grants to law
