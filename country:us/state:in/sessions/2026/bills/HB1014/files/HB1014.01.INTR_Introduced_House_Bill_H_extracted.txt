DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 16-18-2-334.5; IC 16-41-35; IC 35-52-16-76.8.
Synopsis: Sonography. Specifies that the term "radiation" includes
sound waves commonly used for sonography. Prohibits a person from
performing sonography unless certain conditions are met, and provides
both criminal and civil penalties. Makes conforming changes.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Bauer M
December 1, 2025, read first time and referred to Committee on Public Health.

HOUSE BILL No. 1014
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning
health.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 16-18-2-334.5 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA
2 CODE AS A NEW SECTION TO READ AS FOLLOWS
3 [EFFECTIVE JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 334.5. "Sonography services", for
4 purposes of IC 16-41-35, has the meaning set forth in
5 IC 16-41-35-10.5.
6 SECTION 2. IC 16-41-35-8 IS AMENDED TO READ AS
7 FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 8. As used in this
8 chapter, "radiation" means the following:
9 (1) Ionizing radiation, including gamma rays, x-rays, alpha
10 particles, beta particles, and other atomic or nuclear particles or
11 rays.
12 (2) Electromagnetic radiation generated during the operation of
13 electronic products.
14 (3) Sonic, ultrasonic, or infrasonic waves that are emitted from an
15 electronic product as a result of the operation of an electronic
16 circuit in that product that may produce a hazard to health,
17 including sound waves commonly used for sonography.
2026 IN 1014—LS 6128/DI 148
2
1 SECTION 3. IC 16-41-35-10.5 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA
2 CODE AS A NEW SECTION TO READ AS FOLLOWS
3 [EFFECTIVE JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 10.5. As used in this chapter,
4 "sonography services" refers to an imaging technique that uses
5 ultrasonic waves that are emitted from an electronic product to
6 produce images of the inside of a body.
7 SECTION 4. IC 16-41-35-26, AS AMENDED BY P.L.29-2014,
8 SECTION 20, IS AMENDED TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE
9 JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 26. (a) Subject to subsection (b), a person may
10 not:
11 (1) produce radiation; or
12 (2) produce, use, store, sell, or otherwise dispose of:
13 (A) radiation machines; or
14 (B) electronic products;
15 unless the person registers in writing with the state department, giving
16 the pertinent information the state department requires, in accordance
17 with the procedures prescribed by the state department.
18 (b) A person may not provide sonography services unless the
19 person either:
20 (1) is a licensed or certified health care provider under:
21 (A) IC 25-22.5;
22 (B) IC 25-23;
23 (C) IC 25-23.4; or
24 (D) IC 25-27.5; or
25 (2) is providing the sonography services under the supervision
26 of a licensed or certified health care provider described in
27 subdivision (1).
28 (c) A person that violates subsection (b) commits a Class B
29 misdemeanor. Each violation constitutes a separate offense.
30 (d) In addition to any other penalty under this chapter, the state
31 department may impose a civil penalty of not more than
32 twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000) against a person for each
33 violation of subsection (b).
34 SECTION 5. IC 35-52-16-76.8 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA
35 CODE AS A NEW SECTION TO READ AS FOLLOWS
36 [EFFECTIVE JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 76.8. IC 16-41-35-26 defines a
37 crime concerning sonography services.
