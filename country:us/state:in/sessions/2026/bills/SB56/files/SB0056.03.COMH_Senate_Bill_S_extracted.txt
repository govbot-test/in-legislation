DIGEST OF SB 56 (Updated February 5, 2026 9:21 am - DI 140)
Citations Affected: IC 25-38.1.
Synopsis: Veterinary licensing. Provides that the board of veterinary
medicine may issue a license or registration to a qualified applicant
without an examination if the qualified applicant: (1) satisfies the
requirements for reciprocity of health care professionals; and (2)
otherwise meets the requirements to become a licensed veterinarian or
a registered veterinary technician.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Leising, Glick, Yoder, Doriot, Byrne,
Spencer, Tomes, Clark, Schmitt,
Alting, Deery, Becker
(HOUSE SPONSORS — BAIRD, SMITH H)

December 9, 2025, reported favorably — Do Pass.

SENATE BILL No. 56
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning
professions and occupations.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 25-38.1-3-5, AS AMENDED BY P.L.112-2014,
2 SECTION 37, IS AMENDED TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE
3 JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 5. The board may issue a license without an
4 examination to a qualified applicant who:
5 (1) furnishes satisfactory proof that the applicant:
6 (A) is a graduate of an accredited college of veterinary
7 medicine;
8 (B) holds an Educational Commission for Foreign Veterinary
9 Graduates (ECFVG) certificate; or
10 (C) holds a PAVE certificate;
11 (2) for the five (5) years immediately preceding filing an
12 application has been a practicing veterinarian licensed in a state,
13 territory, or district of the United States that has license
14 requirements substantially equivalent to the requirements of this
15 chapter; and
16 (1) satisfies the requirements set forth in IC 25-1-21; and
17 (3) (2) otherwise meets the requirements of this chapter.
ES 56—LS 6307/DI 148
2
1 SECTION 2. IC 25-38.1-3-8, AS AMENDED BY P.L.58-2008,
2 SECTION 33, IS AMENDED TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE
3 JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 8. The board may issue a registration without an
4 examination to a qualified applicant who:
5 (1) furnishes satisfactory proof that the applicant has successfully
6 completed an accredited veterinary technology program;
7 (2) for the five (5) years immediately preceding filing an
8 application has been acting as a registered veterinary technician
9 in a state, territory, or district of the United States that has
10 registration requirements substantially equivalent to the
11 requirements of this chapter; and
12 (1) satisfies the requirements set forth in IC 25-1-21; and
13 (3) (2) otherwise meets the requirements of this chapter.
