4 Chapter 19. Protection of Personal Information
5 Sec. 1. As used in this chapter, "protected individual" means a:
6 (1) current or retired judge (as defined in IC 33-38-12-3);
7 (2) current or retired justice, judge, or magistrate of a federal
8 court domiciled in Indiana; and
9 (3) spouse, child, or dependent who resides in the same
10 household as an individual described in subdivisions (1) and
11 (2).
12 Sec. 2. As used in this chapter, "personal information" includes
13 the following:
14 (1) A home address.
15 (2) A home telephone number.
16 (3) A mobile telephone number.
17 (4) A personal electronic mail address.

14 Sec. 3. As used in this chapter, "publish" means to post or
15 otherwise make available to the general public on the Internet,
16 social media, or social networks.
17 Sec. 4. (a) A protected individual, or the office of judicial
18 administration on behalf of a protected individual, may submit a
19 written request to a public agency (as defined in IC 5-14-3-2(q)) to:
20 (1) prevent publication of personal information of the
21 protected individual; or
22 (2) remove personal information of the protected individual
23 from an existing publication.
24 (b) A request made under subsection (a) must be sent by
25 certified mail or electronic mail and contain the following:
26 (1) Sufficient information to confirm that the person subject
27 to the request is a protected individual.
28 (2) A description of the document, posting, or other
29 publication containing the personal information that is sought
30 to be removed.
31 A request made by the office of judicial administration that
32 certifies that the person subject to the request is a protected
33 individual is sufficient for the purposes of subdivision (1).
34 (c) Not later than one (1) day after receiving a request under
35 this section, a public agency shall acknowledge receipt of the
36 request.
37 (d) After receiving a request under this section, a public agency
38 shall:
39 (1) as soon as practicable, take action necessary to ensure that
40 the personal information of the protected individual is not
41 published; or
42 (2) not later than three (3) days after receipt of the request,
