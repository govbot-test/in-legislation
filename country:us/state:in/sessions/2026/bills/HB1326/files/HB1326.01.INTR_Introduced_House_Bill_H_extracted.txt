DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 2-5-57.5.
Synopsis: Indiana emergency efficiency and resilience task force.
Establishes the Indiana emergency efficiency and resilience task force
(task force). Provides that the task force shall study certain topics
related to disaster resilience. Requires the task force to submit a written
report with specific findings and recommendations to the legislative
council not later than December 1, 2026.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Baird
January 6, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Veterans Affairs and Public
Safety.

A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning the
general assembly.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 2-5-57.5 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA CODE
2 AS A NEW CHAPTER TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE
3 JULY 1, 2026]:
4 Chapter 57.5. Indiana Emergency Efficiency and Resilience
5 Task Force
6 Sec. 1. As used in this chapter, "task force" refers to the Indiana
7 emergency efficiency and resilience task force established by
8 section 2 of this chapter.
9 Sec. 2. The Indiana emergency efficiency and resilience task
10 force is established.
11 Sec. 3. (a) The task force consists of the following members:
12 (1) Five (5) members of the senate, appointed as follows:
13 (A) Three (3) members appointed by the president pro
14 tempore, one (1) of whom serves as co-chair of the task
15 force.
16 (B) Two (2) members appointed by the minority leader.
17 (2) Five (5) members of the house of representatives,

12 (D) A representative from the Indiana department of

14 (E) A representative from the Indiana state department of

16 The members described in subdivisions (1) and (2) are voting

18 (b) A member of the task force serves for the duration of the
19 task force.
20 (c) A vacancy on the task force shall be filled by the appointing

22 Sec. 4. (a) The task force shall meet at the call of the co-chairs.
23 (b) The affirmative votes of a majority of the voting members
24 of the task force are required for the task force to take action on
25 any measure.
26 (c) A quorum of the task force consists of six (6) members.
27 (d) All meetings of the task force are open to the public in

36 general assembly is not entitled to reimbursement for traveling
37 expenses as provided under IC 4-13-1-4 or other expenses actually
38 incurred in connection with the member's duties as provided in the
39 state policies and procedures established by the Indiana

2 Sec. 6. The legislative services agency shall provide staff support
3 to the task force.
4 Sec. 7. (a) The task force shall do the following:
5 (1) Conduct a gap analysis of resilience efforts in Indiana,
6 including:
7 (A) a review of past and current initiatives to increase
8 disaster resilience; and
9 (B) an assessment of data gaps with respect to current and
10 future disaster risks, including data describing
11 precipitation patterns, stream flows, and riverine and flash
12 flood risk.
13 (2) Study the benefit of establishing an office or officer
14 responsible for leading and coordinating resilience efforts in
15 Indiana, including:
16 (A) a review of how other states have implemented or
17 established:
18 (i) state resilience offices;
19 (ii) chief resilience officers; and
20 (iii) state resilience plans; and
21 (B) an assessment of whether the models described in
22 clause (A) may be replicable in Indiana.
23 (3) Assess water management roles within the department of
24 natural resources and department of environmental
25 management to:
26 (A) align responsibilities;
27 (B) consolidate processes where appropriate; and
28 (C) improve statewide operational efficiency.
29 (b) In discharging duties under this section, the task force may
30 hear testimony from professionals, academics, and others with
31 relevant expertise.
32 Sec. 8. Not later than December 1, 2026, the task force shall
33 submit a written report to the legislative council in an electronic
34 format under IC 5-14-6. The report must state specific findings and
35 recommendations regarding the following:
36 (1) What duties are necessary for leading and coordinating
37 resilience efforts in Indiana.
38 (2) Whether a state resilience officer, or equivalent position,
39 should be established to lead and coordinate resilience efforts
40 in Indiana, and where this position should be located within
41 state government.
42 (3) Whether the duties necessary to lead and coordinate

1 resilience efforts in Indiana should be assigned to an existing
2 state agency.
3 (4) Whether a statewide plan for long term disaster resilience
4 and risk reduction should be created.
5 (5) How a statewide plan for long term disaster resilience and
6 risk reduction should be created or incorporated into existing
7 planning processes.
8 (6) What elements a statewide plan for long term disaster
9 resilience and risk reduction should contain.
10 (7) How best to consolidate water responsibilities within state
11 agencies to increase efficiencies.
12 The report may propose legislation or recommend specific
13 executive action.
14 Sec. 9. This chapter expires December 31, 2026.
