1 A HOUSE RESOLUTION honoring the 190th anniversary
2 of the incorporation of Michigan City, Indiana.
3 Whereas, The area now known as Michigan City, Indiana,
4 traces its origins to the early 1830s, when pioneer and founder
5 Isaac C. Elston purchased 160 acres along the southern shore
6 of Lake Michigan and began planning a community rooted in
7 commerce, transportation, and access to the Great Lakes;
8 Whereas, Michigan City was formally incorporated in 1836,
9 with a population of more than 1,500, and has since grown into
10 a vibrant city spanning approximately 15 square miles along
11 the shoreline of Lake Michigan;
12 Whereas, The first mayor of Michigan City, Willys Peck, was
13 elected on April 12, 1836, establishing the foundation of local
14 governance and civic leadership that continues to guide the

2 Whereas, Michigan City's population has grown from just

6 Whereas, Michigan City is home to the Old Michigan City

9 preceded the construction of the Michigan City East Pierhead
10 Light in 1904;
11 Whereas, Both the Old Michigan City Light and the
12 Michigan City East Pierhead Light stand as enduring symbols
13 of the city's maritime heritage and are recognized on the
14 National Register of Historic Places;
15 Whereas, In 1837, Michigan City established a volunteer fire
16 department to protect its residents and businesses. It was later
17 reorganized into a professional department in 1905, with the
18 debut of motorized fire equipment in 1916 marking the city's
19 continued commitment to public safety and innovation;
20 Whereas, Michigan City has played a vital role in preserving
21 and promoting the natural resources of Northwest Indiana,
22 including contributions to protecting and stewarding the
23 Indiana Dunes National Lakeshore, which was redesignated as
24 Indiana Dunes National Park in 2019, and the Indiana Dunes
25 State Park, the most visited state park in Indiana;
26 Whereas, Michigan City provides access to miles of Lake
27 Michigan shoreline, beaches, woodlands, rivers, and wetlands
28 that support a diverse ecosystem of native wildlife and attract
29 visitors from across the nation;
30 Whereas, Michigan City has long served as a regional hub
31 for transportation and commerce, including its historic and
32 ongoing role as a major stop on the South Shore Line, an
33 electric commuter rail system connecting residents and visitors
34 to Chicago, South Bend, and the greater Northwest Indiana
35 region;
