DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 20-34-3-28.5.
Synopsis: Educational information on type 1 diabetes. Requires the
department of education (department) to develop informational
materials concerning type 1 diabetes for the parents of students and to
publish the materials on the department's website. Requires public
schools, including charter schools, to provide the informational
materials concerning type 1 diabetes to a student's parent each year
upon the student's enrollment.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Alting
December 9, 2025, read first time and referred to Committee on Education and Career
Development.

SENATE BILL No. 121
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning
education.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 20-34-3-28.5 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA
2 CODE AS A NEW SECTION TO READ AS FOLLOWS
3 [EFFECTIVE JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 28.5. (a) The department, in
4 coordination with any agency or organization the department
5 deems appropriate, shall develop informational materials
6 concerning type 1 diabetes for the parents of students.
7 (b) The department shall make the informational materials
8 developed under subsection (a) available to public schools,
9 including charter schools, by publishing the informational
10 materials on the department's website.
11 (c) Beginning with the 2026-2027 school year, the governing
12 body of a school corporation or the equivalent authority of a
13 charter school shall make the informational materials described in
14 subsection (a) available to a student's parent each year upon the
15 student's enrollment.
16 (d) The informational materials described in subsection (a) shall
17 include:
2026 IN 121—LS 6469/DI 152
2
1 (1) a description of:
2 (A) type 1 diabetes;
3 (B) risk factors and warning signs associated with type 1
4 diabetes; and
5 (C) the necessity for screening all students for early
6 detection of type 1 diabetes using a blood autoantibody
7 test; and
8 (2) a recommendation for a student displaying:
9 (A) warning signs; or
10 (B) early detection screening results;
11 associated with type 1 diabetes that the student's parent
12 should immediately consult with the student's doctor to
13 develop an appropriate treatment plan, which may include a
14 consultation with a specialty care provider such as a qualified
15 endocrinologist.
