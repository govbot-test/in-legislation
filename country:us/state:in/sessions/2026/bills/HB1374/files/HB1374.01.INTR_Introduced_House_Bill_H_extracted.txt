DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 5-28-6.5.
Synopsis: Requirements for incentive recipient employers. Specifies
additional conditions pertaining to the use of secret ballot elections in
unionization efforts and employee personal contact information that an
employer must comply with to be eligible to receive a tax credit, tax
deduction, grant, loan, or loan guarantee (job creation incentive) from
the Indiana economic development corporation (IEDC). Requires the
IEDC to enter into a separate agreement with a recipient of a job
creation incentive to recover the value of the job creation incentive if
the recipient does not comply with the conditions added by the bill.
Specifies the term of the separate agreement in relation to the value of
the job creation incentive. Requires the IEDC to investigate reports of
noncompliance with the conditions added by the bill during the period
when the separate agreement is in effect and to provide those findings
to the office of the attorney general for the initiation of proceedings for
recovery of job creation incentives.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
VanNatter
January 8, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Employment, Labor and
Pensions.

4 Chapter 6.5. Additional Conditions for the Provision of Job

6 Sec. 1. The following definitions apply throughout this chapter:
7 (1) "Contract" means:
8 (A) an agreement (including any memoranda of
9 understanding) between an employer and the corporation;
10 and
11 (B) an agreement between an employer and a labor
12 organization.
13 (2) "Employee" means an individual who performs services
14 for an employer for wages that are subject to withholding
15 requirements under Section 3402 of the Internal Revenue
16 Code.
17 (3) "Employer" means a business entity that voluntarily

1 pursues job creation incentives or enters into an agreement
2 with the corporation to receive a job creation incentive.
3 (4) "Job creation incentive" has the meaning set forth in
4 IC 5-28-2-5.5.
5 (5) "Labor organization" has the meaning set forth in 29
6 U.S.C. 152.
7 (6) "Neutrality agreement" means an agreement signed with
8 a union in which the employer agrees to conditions including
9 committing not to speak to employees about union issues.
10 (7) "Personal contact information" means an employee's
11 home address, home or personal cellular telephone number,
12 or personal electronic mail address.
13 (8) "Secret ballot election" means a process conducted by the
14 National Labor Relations Board in which an employee casts
15 a secret ballot for or against labor organization
16 representation.
17 (9) "Subcontractor" means an individual or entity that has
18 contracted with the employer to perform work or provide
19 services.
20 Sec. 2. (a) This section does not apply to a contract in effect on
21 June 30, 2026. However, if a contract is amended, renewed, or
22 modified on or after July 1, 2026, the contract must conform to this
23 section.
24 (b) To be eligible to receive a job creation incentive, an
25 employer must not take any of the following actions:
26 (1) Grant recognition rights for employees solely and
27 exclusively on the basis of signed union authorization cards if
28 the selection of a bargaining representative may instead be
29 conducted through a secret ballot election.
30 (2) Voluntarily disclose an employee's personal contact
31 information to a labor organization, or third party acting on
32 behalf of a labor organization, without the employee's prior
33 written consent, unless otherwise required by state or federal
34 law.
35 (3) Sign a neutrality agreement with a labor organization.
36 (4) Require a subcontractor to engage in activities prohibited
37 in subdivision (1).
38 The prohibitions set forth in this subsection apply to any work or
39 service for the employer on the project for which the job creation
40 incentive is provided.
41 (c) The requirements of subsection (b) are in addition to the
42 requirements of IC 5-28-6-6 and any other terms applicable to the

1 job creation incentive.
2 Sec. 3. This section applies to job creation incentives disbursed
3 or provided after June 30, 2026. Before the corporation may
4 provide a job creation incentive to an employer, the corporation
5 must execute a separate agreement with the recipient of the job
6 creation incentive that reserves the right of the corporation to
7 recover the value of the job creation incentive if the recipient
8 benefitting from the job creation incentive fails to comply with
9 section 2 of this chapter. The separate agreement required by this
10 section must have a term of not less than:
11 (1) the term the corporation would require for a job creation
12 incentive of less than five million dollars ($5,000,000); or
13 (2) twenty (20) years, for a job creation incentive of five
14 million dollars ($5,000,000) or more.
15 This section is supplemental to the requirements of IC 5-28-6-6,
16 IC 5-28-28-7, and any other terms applicable to recovery of a job
17 creation incentive.
18 Sec. 4. (a) A person may report, based upon a reasonable belief,
19 a suspected violation of section 2 of this chapter to the corporation.
20 However, the report must be made during the term of the separate
21 agreement entered into between the corporation and the employer
22 under section 3 of this chapter. Upon receiving a report under this
23 section, the corporation shall determine whether a violation has
24 occurred.
25 (b) If the corporation finds that an employer has violated section
26 2 of this chapter, then the corporation shall deliver written notice
27 of the corporation's findings to the:
28 (1) employer; and
29 (2) office of the attorney general to initiate proceedings to
30 recover funds under section 3 of this chapter.
