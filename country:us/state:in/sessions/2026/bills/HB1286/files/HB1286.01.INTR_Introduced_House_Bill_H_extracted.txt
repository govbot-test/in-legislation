DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 34-26-5.
Synopsis: Military protective order. Specifies that a party to a
protective order proceeding has a duty to inform the court of any
military protective orders issued by a military commander in effect
between the parties. Requires the court to consider evidence of a
military protective order in effect against a respondent for the
protection of the petitioner and allows the court to grant an ex parte
order for protection based upon this evidence.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Lauer
January 6, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Judiciary.
