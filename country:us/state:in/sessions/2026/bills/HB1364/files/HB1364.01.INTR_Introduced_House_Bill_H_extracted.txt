DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 16-42-5.7.
Synopsis: Food labeling requirements. Requires a food manufacturer
that offers certain food products for sale in Indiana to ensure that each
food product includes a warning label disclosing specified ingredients
in the food product. Sets forth labeling requirements. Allows the
attorney general to bring an action for a violation of these provisions.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Meltzer, McGuire, Barrett, King
January 8, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Public Health.

HOUSE BILL No. 1364
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning
health.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 16-42-5.7 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA CODE
2 AS A NEW CHAPTER TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE
3 JULY 1, 2026]:
4 Chapter 5.7. Food: Labeling Requirements
5 Sec. 1. (a) This chapter applies to a food product label:
6 (1) printed; or
7 (2) copyrighted;
8 after June 30, 2027.
9 (b) This chapter does not apply to any of the following offered
10 for sale in Indiana by a food manufacturer:
11 (1) A product that contains an ingredient not intended for
12 human consumption.
13 (2) Food labeled, prepared, served, or sold in the following:
14 (A) A food establishment.
15 (B) A retail establishment.
16 (3) A product regulated by the federal Department of
17 Agriculture Food Safety and Inspection Service.

1 (4) A product labeled with a warning from the Surgeon
2 General of the federal Public Health Service.
3 (5) A drug.
4 (6) A dietary supplement.
5 (7) A pesticide chemical, soil, plant nutrient, or other raw
6 agricultural commodity.
7 Sec. 2. (a) If the federal Food and Drug Administration requires
8 an ingredient to be named on a food product label and the food
9 manufacturer offers the food product for sale in Indiana, the food
10 manufacturer shall ensure that each food product intended for
11 human consumption includes a warning label that discloses the use
12 of any of the following ingredients in the food product:

1 Potassium iodate.

17 (b) The warning label required under subsection (a) must meet
18 the following requirements:
19 (1) Include the following statement:
20 "WARNING: This product contains an ingredient that is
21 not recommended for human consumption by the
22 appropriate authority in Australia, Canada, the European
23 Union, or the United Kingdom.".
24 (2) Be printed in a font size not smaller than the smallest font
25 size used to disclose other consumer information required by
26 the federal Food and Drug Administration.
27 (3) Contains sufficiently high contrast font to ensure the
28 statement described in subdivision (1) is likely to be seen and
29 understood by the intended consumer.
30 Sec. 3. If a food manufacturer or retailer offers a food product
31 described in section 2 of this chapter for sale in Indiana on the
32 manufacturer's or retailer's website, the food manufacturer or the
33 retailer shall disclose to the consumer all labeling information
34 required under section 2 of this chapter by either:
35 (1) posting a legible statement on the website on which the
36 product is offered for sale; or
37 (2) communicating the information to the consumer in some
38 other manner.
39 Sec. 4. If a food manufacturer violates this chapter, the attorney
40 general may bring an action to obtain any of the following against
41 the food manufacturer:
42 (1) Injunctive relief.

4
1 (2) A civil penalty of not more than fifty thousand dollars
2 ($50,000) per day for each violation.
3 (3) The attorney general's reasonable costs of:
4 (A) the investigation of the violation; and
5 (B) maintaining the action.
6 (4) Other appropriate relief.
7 Sec. 5. A violation of this chapter does not create a private cause
8 of action.
