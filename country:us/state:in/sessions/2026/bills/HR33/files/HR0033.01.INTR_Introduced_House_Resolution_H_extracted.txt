1 A HOUSE RESOLUTION recognizing March 2026 as
2 Brain Injury Awareness Month in Indiana.
3 Whereas, Every nine seconds, someone in the United States
4 sustains an acquired brain injury, traumatic or nontraumatic,
5 from a fall, stroke, accident, infection, lack of oxygen, sports
6 activities, or military service;
7 Whereas, At least 64 million adults report having
8 experienced one or more traumatic brain injuries in their
9 lifetime;

1 Whereas, Over 11 million Americans over the age of 40 who
2 have experienced a traumatic brain injury with loss of
3 consciousness are living with a disability;
4 Whereas, Brain injury and head trauma have been linked to
5 the development of neurodegenerative disease and other
6 conditions including but not limited to Parkinson's disease,
7 ischemic stroke, Alzheimer's disease, chronic traumatic
8 encephalopathy, and depression;
9 Whereas, Estimates by the Centers for Disease Control and
10 Prevention and the recently updated and analyzed Indiana
11 census suggest there are approximately 1.6 million individuals
12 who have experienced a brain injury from trauma or stroke in
13 the state, and that nearly 300,000 Hoosiers are disabled as a
14 result of those events;
15 Whereas, The Indiana Department of Health reported that
16 traumatic brain injury contributes to 30 percent of all
17 injury-related deaths in the state, and that 6,726 Hoosiers
18 experienced traumatic brain injury-related hospitalization in
19 2016, 58 percent of whom resided within rural counties;
20 Whereas, Many brain injuries go unreported and untreated
21 because of a lack of education and awareness and a lack of
22 recognition of the effects of brain injury on survivors and
23 families;
24 Whereas, Brain injury survivors and their families can
25 struggle to identify and secure proper medical treatment,
26 services, and care, despite the often lifelong effects of brain
27 injuries; and
28 Whereas, The Brain Injury Association of Indiana works to
29 reduce the incidence and impact of brain injury through
30 education, advocacy, support, and prevention, and by
31 facilitating interagency commitment and collaboration that
32 flows from greater public understanding of brain injuries:
33 Therefore,
34 Be it resolved by the House of Representatives of the
35 General Assembly of the State of Indiana:

1 SECTION 1. That the Indiana House of Representatives
2 recognizes March 2026 as Brain Injury Awareness Month in
3 Indiana to support brain injury survivors and their families,
4 increase the public's awareness of the prevalence of brain injury
5 in the state and nationally, and recognize the importance of
6 prevention and treatment of brain injuries.
7 SECTION 2. That the Principal Clerk of the House of
8 Representatives shall transmit copies of this resolution to State
