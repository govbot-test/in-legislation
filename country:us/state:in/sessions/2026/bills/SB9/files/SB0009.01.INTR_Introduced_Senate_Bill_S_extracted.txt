SENATE BILL No. 9
_____
DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 35-35-3-5; IC 35-38-1-12; IC 35-40-5-5.
Synopsis: Victim impact statements at sentencing. Requires a
defendant to be present in the courtroom while a victim makes a
statement concerning the crime and the sentence unless the defendant
presents a safety risk or causes a significant disruption.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Brown L
December 8, 2025, read first time and referred to Committee on Corrections and Criminal
Law.

4 certify that he the prosecuting attorney has offered to show the

8 (b) A victim present at sentencing in a felony or misdemeanor case

11 victim, if present, an opportunity to make a statement concerning the
12 crime and the sentence. If unable to attend the hearing, the victim may
13 mail a written statement to the court, which must be included in the
14 presentence report made with respect to the defendant.
15 (c) However, this section gives no additional rights to the defendant.
16 Failure to comply gives no grounds for postconviction relief.
