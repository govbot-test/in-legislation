DIGEST OF SB 9 (Updated January 13, 2026 11:33 am - DI 106)
Citations Affected: IC 35-35; IC 35-38; IC 35-40.
Synopsis: Victim impact statements at sentencing. Requires a
defendant to be present in the courtroom while a victim makes a
statement concerning the crime and the sentence unless the defendant
presents a safety risk, causes a significant disruption, was tried in
absentia, or fails to appear.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Brown L, Johnson T, Bassler
December 8, 2025, read first time and referred to Committee on Corrections and Criminal
Law.
January 13, 2026, amended, reported favorably — Do Pass.

SENATE BILL No. 9
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning
criminal law and procedure.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 35-35-3-5 IS AMENDED TO READ AS
2 FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 5. (a) As a part of the
3 recommendation submitted to the court, the prosecuting attorney must
4 certify that he the prosecuting attorney has offered to show the
5 proposed recommendation to the victims of the felony, if any, and that
6 they have been offered an opportunity to present their opinion of the
7 recommendation to the prosecuting attorney and the court.
8 (b) A victim present at sentencing in a felony or misdemeanor case
9 shall be advised by the court of a victim's right to make a statement
10 concerning the crime and the sentence. The court shall also offer the
11 victim, if present, an opportunity to make a statement concerning the
12 crime and the sentence. If unable to attend the hearing, the victim may
13 mail a written statement to the court, which must be included in the
14 presentence report made with respect to the defendant.
15 (c) However, this section gives no additional rights to the defendant.
16 Failure to comply gives no grounds for postconviction relief.
17 (d) Except as provided in subsection (e), the defendant shall be

1 present in the courtroom while a victim makes a statement
2 concerning the crime and the sentence under this section.
3 (e) A court may only excuse a defendant from being in the
4 courtroom while a victim makes a statement if the defendant
5 presents a safety risk or causes a significant disruption. However,
6 this section does not prohibit a court from sentencing a defendant
7 who is absent from the courtroom if the defendant was tried in
8 absentia or if the defendant fails to appear.
9 SECTION 2. IC 35-38-1-12 IS AMENDED TO READ AS
10 FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 12. (a) Before imposing
11 sentence, the court shall:
12 (1) advise the defendant or his the defendant's counsel and the
13 prosecuting attorney of the factual contents and conclusions of the
14 presentence investigation; or
15 (2) provide the defendant or his the defendant's counsel and the
16 prosecuting attorney with a copy of the presentence report.
17 The court also shall offer the victim, if present, an opportunity to make
18 a statement concerning the crime and the sentence. Except as
19 provided in subsection (c), the defendant shall be present in the
20 courtroom while a victim makes a statement concerning the crime
21 and the sentence under this subsection.
22 (b) The sources of confidential information need not be disclosed.
23 The court shall furnish the factual contents of the presentence
24 investigation or a copy of the presentence report sufficiently in advance
25 of sentencing so that the defendant will be afforded a fair opportunity
26 to controvert the material included.
27 (c) A court may only excuse a defendant from being in the
28 courtroom while a victim makes a statement if the defendant
29 presents a safety risk or causes a significant disruption. However,
30 this section does not prohibit a court from sentencing a defendant
31 who is absent from the courtroom if the defendant was tried in
32 absentia or if the defendant fails to appear.
33 SECTION 3. IC 35-40-5-5 IS AMENDED TO READ AS
34 FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 5. (a) A victim has the
35 right to be heard at any proceeding involving sentencing, a
36 postconviction release decision, or a pre-conviction release decision
37 under a forensic diversion program.
38 (b) A victim has the right to make a statement concerning the
39 crime and the defendant's sentence in the presence of the defendant
40 unless the defendant:
41 (1) presents a safety risk;
42 (2) causes a significant disruption;
