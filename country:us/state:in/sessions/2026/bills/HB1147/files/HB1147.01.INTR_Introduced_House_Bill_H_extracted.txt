DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 25-2.1-3.
Synopsis: Licensure of certified public accountants. Provides that an
individual is eligible to take the examination for licensure as a certified
public accountant if the individual has at least a baccalaureate degree
with a concentration in accounting or equivalent as determined by the
board to be appropriate.
Effective: July 1, 2026; January 1, 2027.
VanNatter
January 5, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Employment, Labor and
Pensions.

A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning
professions and occupations.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 25-2.1-3-2, AS AMENDED BY P.L.59-2021,
2 SECTION 1, IS AMENDED TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE
3 JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 2. (a) A first time examination candidate must
4 have at least:
5 (1) one hundred twenty (120) semester hours (or the equivalent if
6 a different grading period is used) of college education that
7 includes an accounting concentration or equivalent as determined
8 by the board to be appropriate; or
9 (2) a baccalaureate degree with a concentration in accounting
10 or equivalent as determined by the board to be appropriate.
11 (b) A candidate for an initial issuance of a certificate must have:
12 (1) at least one hundred fifty (150) semester hours (or the
13 equivalent if a different grading period is used) of college
14 education; and
15 (2) a baccalaureate or higher degree conferred by a college or
16 university acceptable to the board. The educational program must
17 include an accounting concentration or equivalent as determined

21 concentration in accounting, conferred by a college or university
22 as deemed acceptable by the board.
