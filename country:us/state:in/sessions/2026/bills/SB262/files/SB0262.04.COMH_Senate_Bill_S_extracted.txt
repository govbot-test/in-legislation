DIGEST OF SB 262 (Updated February 17, 2026 11:29 am - DI 147)
Citations Affected: IC 25-26.
Synopsis: INSPECT program. Sets forth the circumstances in which
a dispenser is not required to transmit certain information to the
INSPECT program (program). Requires an opioid treatment program
to, before reporting patient information to the program, comply with
federal regulations in obtaining patient consent. Allows the board of
pharmacy to disclose prescription drug monitoring program data to a
representative of the Indiana department of health and the office of the
secretary of family and social services for specified purposes.
Effective: July 1, 2026.

INSPECT program (program). Requires an opioid treatment program
to, before reporting patient information to the program, comply with
federal regulations in obtaining patient consent. Allows the board of
pharmacy to disclose prescription drug monitoring program data to a
representative of the Indiana department of health and the office of the
secretary of family and social services for specified purposes.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
