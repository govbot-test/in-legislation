Section 1:
PRINTING CODE. Amendments: Whenever an existing statute (or a section of the Indiana
Constitution) is being amended, the text of the existing provision will appear in this style type,
additions will appear in this style type, and deletions will appear in this style type.
Additions: Whenever a new statutory provision is being enacted (or a new constitutional
provision adopted), the text of the new provision will appear in this style type. Also, the
word NEW will appear in that style type in the introductory clause of each SECTION that adds
a new provision to the Indiana Code or the Indiana Constitution.
Conflict reconciliation: Text in a statute in this style type or this style type reconciles conflicts
between statutes enacted by the 2025 Regular Session of the General Assembly.
HOUSE ENROLLED ACT No. 1029
AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning health.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:

Section 2:
SECTION 1. IC 16-41-18.2 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA CODE
AS A NEW CHAPTER TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE
JULY 1, 2026]:
Chapter 18.2. Alzheimer's Disease and Dementia Education
Sec. 1. (a) The state department shall collaborate with a national
Alzheimer's disease and dementia organization in educating the
public about the following concerning Alzheimer's disease and
dementia:
(1) The signs and symptoms of Alzheimer's disease and
dementia.
(2) Personal risk factors.
(3) Options for diagnosing and treating Alzheimer's disease
and dementia.
(4) Populations with elevated risk for Alzheimer's disease and
dementia.
(b) The state department shall identify additional partners to
collaborate with in educating the public about Alzheimer's disease
and dementia, including any of the following:
(1) Local health departments.
(2) The division of aging.
(3) Employer wellness programs.
(4) Health care providers, including hospitals.

(5) Health insurers.
(6) Nonprofit organizations.
(7) Community organizations.
Sec. 2. The state department shall partner for outreach with a
national Alzheimer's disease and dementia organization that has
an initiative or program to educate and train health care providers
and human services providers on the following current and
accurate scientific and medical information topics concerning
Alzheimer's disease and dementia:
(1) Diagnosis.
(2) Treatment.
(3) Research advances.
(4) Care planning.
Sec. 3. The state department shall publish information described
in section 2 of this chapter on the state department's website.
Sec. 4. The state department may accept grants, services, and
property from the federal government, public entities, and private
entities to provide the education described in this chapter. The
state department may seek any federal waiver needed to maximize
federal funds to educate the public about Alzheimer's disease and
dementia.
