DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 14-18-13.
Synopsis: Studies relating to natural lands. Requires the department
of natural resources to study and issue a report on the following topics:
(1) the impact of natural lands on public health; (2) options for a
consistent visitor fee collection system at state fish and wildlife areas;
and (3) a plan to mitigate and recover from natural disasters affecting
public lands.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Yoder
December 8, 2025, read first time and referred to Committee on Natural Resources.

SENATE BILL No. 67
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning
natural and cultural resources.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 14-18-13 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA CODE
2 AS A NEW CHAPTER TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE
3 JULY 1, 2026]:
4 Chapter 13. Indiana Public Land Study
5 Sec. 1. As used in this chapter, "public health indicators"
6 means:
7 (1) physical activity;
8 (2) mental health; and
9 (3) community well-being.
10 Sec. 2. As used in this chapter, "public land" refers to Indiana's:
11 (1) state parks;
12 (2) state forests;
13 (3) fish and wildlife areas; and
14 (4) nature preserves.
15 Sec. 3. The department shall conduct and prepare a study of the
16 following topics:
17 (1) The relationship between:

6 (3) Options for implementing a consistent and efficient visitor
7 fee collection system at state fish and wildlife areas, including:

1 (ii) federal assistance;

4 Sec. 4. (a) In carrying out the study under section 3 of this
5 chapter, the department shall, to the greatest extent practicable,
6 use existing:
7 (1) data;
8 (2) analyses;
9 (3) partnerships; and
10 (4) staff capacity.
11 (b) The department may consult with:
12 (1) the Indiana department of health;
13 (2) the Indiana University School of Public Health;
14 (3) Purdue University;

17 (6) other relevant stakeholders;

20 recommendations under section 3 of this chapter to the:
21 (1) governor;
22 (2) interim study committee on agriculture and natural
23 resources; and
24 (3) legislative council;
25 in an electronic format under IC 5-14-6 not later than November
26 1, 2027.
27 (b) The report required under subsection (a) may be
28 incorporated into or transmitted concurrently with any other
29 statutorily required report of the department to minimize
30 administrative burden.
31 Sec. 6. (a) The department shall carry out this chapter using
32 existing appropriations, federal funds, grants, and partnerships,
33 without requiring additional state general fund appropriations.
34 (b) This chapter does not:
35 (1) create a private right of action; or
36 (2) authorize new fees or taxes.
37 Recommendations regarding visitor fees at state fish and wildlife
38 areas shall be submitted to the general assembly for consideration
39 before implementation.
40 Sec. 7. This chapter expires July 1, 2028.
