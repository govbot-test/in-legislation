DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 20-28-5-12.
Synopsis: Teacher licensing requirements. Provides that certain
individuals seeking an initial practitioner license are exempt from
certain licensing requirements.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Criswell, Morris
January 8, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Education.

HOUSE BILL No. 1418
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning
education.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 20-28-5-12, AS AMENDED BY P.L.194-2025,
2 SECTION 1, AND P.L.214-2025, SECTION 112, IS AMENDED TO
3 READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 12. (a)
4 Subsection (b) does not apply to an individual who:
5 (1) held an Indiana limited, reciprocal, or standard teaching
6 license on June 30, 1985; or
7 (2) is granted a license under section 12.5, 16.5, or 18 of this
8 chapter.
9 (b) Except as provided in subsection (f) and sections 12.5 and 16.5
10 of this chapter, the department may not grant an initial practitioner
11 license to an individual unless the individual has:
12 (1) met the requirements of section 12.3 of this chapter; and
13 (2) demonstrated proficiency in the following areas on a written
14 examination or through other procedures prescribed by the
15 department:
16 (A) Pedagogy.
17 (B) Knowledge of the areas in which the individual is required
2026 IN 1418—LS 6261/DI 152
2
1 to have a license to teach.
2 (C) If the individual is seeking to be licensed as an elementary
3 school teacher, comprehensive scientifically based reading
4 instruction skills aligned to the science of reading.
5 (c) An individual's license examination score may not be disclosed
6 by the department without the individual's consent unless specifically
7 required by state or federal statute or court order.
8 (d) Subject to section 22 of this chapter, the state board shall adopt
9 rules under IC 4-22-2 to do the following:
10 (1) Adopt, validate, and implement the examination or other
11 procedures required by subsection (b).
12 (2) Establish examination scores indicating proficiency.
13 (3) Otherwise carry out the purposes of this section.
14 (e) Subject to section 18 of this chapter, the state board shall adopt
15 rules under IC 4-22-2 establishing the conditions under which the
16 requirements of this section may be waived for an individual holding
17 a valid teacher's license issued by another state.
18 (f) An individual seeking an initial practitioner license under
19 this chapter is exempt from the requirements under subsection
20 (b)(2)(B) if the individual meets the following criteria:
21 (1) The individual holds at least a bachelor's degree with a
22 grade point average of at least 3.0 on a 4.0 point scale from an
23 accredited postsecondary institution in the content or a
24 related area in which the individual wishes to teach.
25 (2) The individual is seeking a license in any of the following
26 content areas:
27 (A) Physical education, music, or art for a kindergarten
28 through grade 12 license.
29 (B) English/language arts, mathematics, reading, or health
30 for a grade 7 through grade 12 license.
