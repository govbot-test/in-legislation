DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 8-1-46.1; IC 13-11-2-242; IC 13-18-12-7.5.
Synopsis: Regulation of grease control equipment. Establishes a
statewide regulatory scheme for grease control equipment. Provides
that a wastewater treatment facility shall accept septage from within the
unit or a bordering unit.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Pressel, Prescott, Morris
January 6, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Environmental Affairs.

3 JULY 1, 2026]:
4 Chapter 46.1. Grease Control
5 Sec. 1. This chapter applies to the following:
6 (1) A commercial facility that discharges wastewater
7 containing fats, oils, or grease into a sewer system.
8 (2) A food establishment (as defined in IC 16-18-2-137).
9 (3) A mobile retail food establishment (as defined in
10 IC 16-18-2-239.3).
11 Sec. 2. As used in this chapter, "fats, oils, and grease" or "FOG"
12 means any organic substance derived from animal or vegetable
13 sources that may:
14 (1) solidify;
15 (2) congeal; or
16 (3) become viscous at ambient temperatures;
17 and can obstruct, accumulate, or interfere in a wastewater

1 collection or a sewer system.
2 Sec. 3. As used in this chapter, "grease control equipment"
3 refers to the necessary combination of:
4 (1) grease interceptors; and
5 (2) grease traps;
6 to prevent fats, oils, grease, and food solids from being discharged
7 into a sewer system.
8 Sec. 4. As used in this chapter, "grease hauler" means a person
9 or company regulated and permitted by the Indiana department of
10 environmental management and that engages in the:
11 (1) collection;
12 (2) transport; or
13 (3) disposal;
14 of material removed from a grease trap or grease interceptor.
15 Sec. 5. As used in this chapter, "grease interceptor" means an
16 exterior or underground watertight receptacle that is designed to
17 intercept and retain:
18 (1) fats;
19 (2) oils;
20 (3) grease; and
21 (4) food solids;
22 from high volume wastewater flows before the wastewater is
23 discharged into a sewer system.
24 Sec. 6. As used in this chapter, "grease trap" means a watertight
25 tank designed to:
26 (1) intercept;
27 (2) congeal; and
28 (3) retain or remove;
29 fats, oils, and grease from wastewater before the wastewater is
30 discharged into a sewer system.
31 Sec. 7. As used in this chapter, "nonresidential onsite sewage
32 system" has the meaning set forth in IC 16-19-3.5-3.5.
33 Sec. 8. As used in this chapter, "properly sized" refers to the
34 sizing and configuration of a grease trap or grease interceptor in
35 accordance with:
36 (1) good engineering practice;
37 (2) state plumbing codes; and
38 (3) manufacturer specifications;
39 sufficient to prevent fats, oils, and grease from entering a sewer
40 system or a nonresidential onsite sewage system in quantities that
41 cause blockages, interference, or maintenance problems.
42 Sec. 9. As used in this chapter, "regulated entity" means a:

2 fats, oils, or grease into a sewer system;
3 (2) food establishment (as defined in IC 16-18-2-137); or

5 IC 16-18-2-239.3).
6 Sec. 10. As used in this chapter, "regulating authority" means
7 the entity having jurisdictional oversight authority over the
8 regulated entity.
9 Sec. 11. As used in this chapter, "sewer system" has the meaning
10 set forth in IC 36-9-22.5-3.
11 Sec. 12. As used in this chapter, "utility" means:
12 (1) a public utility (as defined in IC 8-1-2-1(a));
13 (2) a municipally owned utility (as defined in IC 8-1-2-1(h));
14 (3) a not-for-profit utility (as defined in IC 8-1-2-125(a));
15 (4) a cooperatively owned corporation;
16 (5) a conservancy district established under IC 14-33; or
17 (6) a regional district established under IC 13-26;
18 that provides sewage disposal service to the public.
19 Sec. 13. (a) A regulated entity shall install and operate properly
20 sized grease control equipment to prevent the discharge of
21 wastewater containing fats, oils, or grease in concentrations that
22 cause or contribute to:
23 (1) blockages;
24 (2) overflows; or
25 (3) interference;
26 in a sewer system.
27 (b) The regulated entity's grease control equipment shall be:
28 (1) designed; or
29 (2) approved;
30 by a licensed professional engineer under IC 25-31 or a
31 professional architect under IC 25-4.
32 (c) A regulated entity's grease control equipment shall be
33 installed by a licensed plumber.
34 (d) A regulated entity that demonstrates through operational
35 characteristics, inspection, or sampling that the regulated entity
36 does not generate fats, oils, or grease in quantities requiring
37 interception is:
38 (1) exempt from the requirements of this chapter; and
39 (2) not required to install grease control equipment.
40 Sec. 14. A regulated entity's compliance with this chapter is
41 demonstrated through the installation, maintenance, and operation
42 of grease control equipment in compliance with best practices that

1 meet applicable:
2 (1) state plumbing code requirements;
3 (2) manufacturer specifications;
4 (3) equipment performance standards; and
5 (4) sizing formulas adopted by the regulating authority.
6 Sec. 15. A regulated entity complying with this chapter shall be
7 charged the same base sewer rate as non-FOG commercial users.
8 Sec. 16. (a) The regulating authority shall evaluate a regulated
9 entity's compliance with this chapter based on the regulated
10 entity's actual discharge of FOG into a sewer system.
11 (b) A regulating authority shall consider the:
12 (1) size;
13 (2) products; and
14 (3) operational characteristics;
15 of each regulated entity when determining maintenance frequency,
16 inspection schedules, and rate classifications.

18 noncompliance resulting in:
19 (1) excessive FOG discharge; or
20 (2) significant sewer system impact;
21 documented through inspection or testing by the regulating
22 authority.
23 Sec. 17. (a) A regulating authority may not recommend and a
24 utility may not impose higher sewer rates or surcharges on a
25 regulated entity solely because of the regulated entity's business
26 type if the regulated entity:
27 (1) maintains an approved grease control program; and
28 (2) can demonstrate compliance with the performance
29 standard through maintenance records or testing.
30 (b) A utility or regulating authority may not:
31 (1) impose a blanket grease control equipment mandate; or
32 (2) require the:
33 (A) installation of a specific type;
34 (B) installation of a specific size; or
35 (C) specific location;
36 of a grease trap or grease interceptor if a regulated entity has the
37 properly sized and maintained grease control equipment that
38 prevents FOG discharges exceeding the allowable concentration.
39 (c) A regulating authority or utility may not require the
40 following:
41 (1) A specific type of grease control equipment.
42 (2) The specific placement of grease control equipment on the

1 property of a regulated entity.
2 Sec. 18. (a) Before construction or a major renovation of a
3 regulated entity, the owner of the regulated entity or the owner's
4 agent shall submit the proposed grease control equipment design
5 to the applicable regulating authority for review before
6 installation.
7 (b) Upon receiving the grease control equipment design
8 described in subsection (a), the regulating authority shall review
9 the proposed design and provide:
10 (1) a written approval;
11 (2) a written denial; or
12 (3) modification recommendations;
13 based on performance standards under this chapter within ten (10)
14 business days.
15 (c) A regulating authority shall coordinate with the appropriate:
16 (1) building and zoning authority;
17 (2) plumbing permit authorities; or
18 (3) utility;
19 to ensure the regulated entity receives consistent direction before
20 construction.
21 (d) A regulating authority that determines a regulated entity's
22 proposed:
23 (1) grease control equipment design; or
24 (2) maintenance plan;
25 does not meet the performance standard shall provide a written
26 explanation to the regulated entity.
27 Sec. 19. (a) A regulated entity that installs grease control
28 equipment consistent with a regulating authority's written
29 approval is considered compliant upon installation.
30 (b) A regulating authority or utility may not require the
31 regulated entity to retrofit or replace grease control equipment
32 unless the regulated entity's FOG discharge continuously exceeds
33 the allowable concentration.
34 (c) The regulating authority and utility shall notify the regulated
35 entity of noncompliance through written notice within forty-eight
36 (48) hours of the utility learning of the regulated entity's
37 noncompliance.
38 Sec. 20. A regulating authority and utility shall permit a
39 regulated entity to comply with this chapter through alternative
40 grease control equipment or maintenance protocols approved by
41 the regulating authority where installation of an exterior grease
42 interceptor is impractical due to:

1 (1) the available space;
2 (2) the structure; or
3 (3) other site constraints.
4 Sec. 21. A regulating authority shall maintain a FOG control
5 and maintenance program. The program must include the
6 following:
7 (1) A registration or permit system linking each regulated
8 entity to the regulated entity's:
9 (A) grease control equipment; and
10 (B) maintenance plan.
11 (2) A maintenance rule requiring cleaning in accordance with
12 a regularly scheduled maintenance protocol approved by a
13 regulating authority.
14 (3) Clear documentation procedures requiring a regulated
15 entity using a grease hauler to maintain records provided to
16 the regulated entity by a grease hauler that describes the:
17 (A) date of each haul;
18 (B) volume of FOG removed; and
19 (C) grease hauler's name.
20 (4) Grease hauler accountability standards requiring:
21 (A) before and after photographs of grease control
22 equipment;
23 (B) proof of proper disposal by the grease hauler; and
24 (C) a grease hauler to maintain all records of each dump
25 for two (2) years and produce the records upon request of
26 a regulating authority or the Indiana department of
27 environmental management.
28 (5) Inspection and enforcement procedures, including:
29 (A) periodic inspections;
30 (B) review of maintenance records; and
31 (C) a graduated enforcement system for regulated entities
32 that continuously violate this chapter.
33 (6) A variance and appeal process allowing regulated entities
34 to demonstrate compliance through alternative methods
35 certified by a:
36 (A) licensed professional engineer under IC 25-31; or
37 (B) professional architect under IC 25-4.
38 (7) Educational materials or templates provided to regulated
39 entities describing best management practices for grease
40 control equipment.
41 Sec. 22. (a) A regulated entity shall do the following:
42 (1) Install, operate, and maintain grease control equipment

7
1 designed to prevent fats, oils, and grease from entering the
2 wastewater system or a nonresidential onsite sewage system.
3 (2) Ensure that grease control equipment is properly sized
4 and maintained in compliance with section 13 of this chapter.
5 (3) Maintain records that include the:
6 (A) date of each haul;
7 (B) volume of FOG removed; and
8 (C) grease hauler's name.
9 (b) A regulated entity shall maintain all records under this
10 section for two (2) years.
11 (c) A regulated entity that cleans its own grease control
12 equipment is not required to keep records unless the regulated
13 entity is found to produce FOG discharge exceeding the allowable
14 concentration. Upon the failure of a regulated entity to maintain
15 the allowable FOG concentration, the regulating authority shall
16 notify the regulated entity to begin maintaining full cleaning logs
17 and documentation. Once the regulated entity comes back into
18 compliance with this chapter or the standards set by the regulating
19 authority, the regulated entity is no longer required to maintain
20 records.
21 Sec. 23. (a) A grease hauler shall do the following:
22 (1) Take before and after time stamped photographs of each
23 service of a regulated entity.
24 (2) Maintain the time stamped photographs and share the
25 time stamped photographs with the Indiana department of
26 environmental management if requested by the Indiana
27 department of environmental management.
28 (3) Maintain records showing that the total volume of FOG
29 pumped from a regulated entity matches the total disposed of
30 by the grease hauler.
31 (4) Provide documentation or an invoice to a regulated entity
32 after service is completed with the following information:
33 (A) The date of the haul.
34 (B) The volume of FOG removed.
35 (C) The grease hauler's name.
36 (b) The Indiana department of environmental management may
37 seek an injunction to prohibit a grease hauler that has violated this
38 chapter more than three (3) times from operating within Indiana.
