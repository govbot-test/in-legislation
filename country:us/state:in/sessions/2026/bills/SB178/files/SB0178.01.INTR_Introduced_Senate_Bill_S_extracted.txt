SENATE BILL No. 178
_____
DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 27-8-5.7; IC 27-13-36.2.
Synopsis: Payment of insurance claims. Provides that an insurer may
not adjust a subsequent claim filed by a provider as a method of
obtaining reimbursement of an overpayment made by the insurer on a
prior claim by the provider. Provides that a health maintenance
organization may not adjust a subsequent claim filed by a provider as
a method of obtaining reimbursement of an overpayment made by the
health maintenance organization on a prior claim by the provider.
Provides that an insurer may not, more than one year after the date on
which an overpayment on a provider claim was made to the provider
by the insurer, request that the provider repay the overpayment (current
law is two years). Provides that a health maintenance organization may
not, more than one year after the date on which an overpayment on a
provider claim was made to the provider by the health maintenance
organization, request that the provider repay the overpayment (current
law is two years). Makes conforming changes.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Jackson L
January 5, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Insurance and Financial
Institutions.
