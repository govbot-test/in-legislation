DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 36-1-3-15.6.
Synopsis: Residency of prosecutors and public defenders. Provides
that a deputy prosecuting attorney and a public defender may not be
subject to a county residency requirement, unless the residency
requirement includes all counties contiguous to the county in which the
individual serves.
Effective: Upon passage.
Zimmerman, McNamara
December 1, 2025, read first time and referred to Committee on Courts and Criminal Code.

HOUSE BILL No. 1025
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning local
government.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 36-1-3-15.6 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA CODE
2 AS A NEW SECTION TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE
3 UPON PASSAGE]: Sec. 15.6. (a) The following may not be subject
4 to a county residency requirement, unless the residency
5 requirement includes all counties contiguous to the county in which
6 the individual serves:
7 (1) A deputy prosecuting attorney.
8 (2) A public defender.
9 (b) This section does not apply to a chief public defender.
10 SECTION 2. An emergency is declared for this act.
