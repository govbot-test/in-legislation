DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 36-8.
Synopsis: 911 telecommunications fees. Increases the: (1) 911 service
prepaid wireless charge; and (2) monthly statewide 911 fee; from $1 to
$2.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Spencer
January 8, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Homeland Security and
Transportation.

A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning
utilities.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 36-8-16.6-11, AS AMENDED BY P.L.119-2024,
2 SECTION 7, IS AMENDED TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE
3 JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 11. (a) The board shall impose a 911 service
4 prepaid wireless charge on each retail transaction. The charge is not
5 required to be paid by an eligible telecommunications carrier that is
6 required to pay the monthly statewide 911 fee under IC 36-8-16.7-32
7 for the same transaction. The amount of the charge is one dollar ($1).
8 two dollars ($2). The board may increase the 911 service prepaid
9 wireless charge to ensure adequate revenue for the board to fulfill its
10 duties and obligations under this chapter and IC 36-8-16.7. The
11 following apply to an increase in the 911 service prepaid wireless
12 charge:
13 (1) The board may increase the charge only one (1) time after
14 April 30, 2023, and before July 1, 2026, in an amount not to
15 exceed ten cents ($0.10).
16 (2) The board may increase the charge only after review by the
17 budget committee.

1 (b) A consumer that is the federal government or an agency of the
2 federal government is exempt from the 911 service prepaid wireless
3 charge imposed under this section.
4 (c) This subsection applies to an eligible telecommunications carrier
5 for purposes of receiving Lifeline reimbursement from the universal
6 service fund through the administrator designated by the Federal
7 Communications Commission. An eligible telecommunications carrier:
8 (1) is not considered an agency of the federal government for
9 purposes of the exemption set forth in subsection (b); and
10 (2) with respect to prepaid wireless telecommunications service
11 provided to end users by the eligible telecommunications carrier
12 in its capacity as an eligible telecommunications carrier, is liable
13 for the charge imposed under subsection (d).
14 (d) Beginning September 1, 2015, and on the first day of each
15 month thereafter, an eligible telecommunications carrier described in
16 subsection (c) shall pay to the board a charge equal to the product of
17 the following factors:
18 (1) The 911 service prepaid wireless charge established under
19 subsection (a).
20 (2) The number of unique end users for which the eligible
21 telecommunications carrier received reimbursement from the
22 universal service fund during the immediately preceding month.
23 The eligible telecommunications carrier may bill and collect from each
24 end user the charges calculated under this subsection with respect to
25 the end user. The eligible telecommunications carrier shall determine
26 the manner in which the eligible telecommunications carrier bills and
27 collects the charges. Except as provided in section 15 of this chapter,
28 an eligible telecommunications carrier may not bill and collect from an
29 end user an amount greater than the charges paid by the eligible
30 telecommunications carrier to the board with respect to the end user.
31 (e) If the board increases the 911 service prepaid wireless charge
32 under subsection (a), the board shall provide written notice to the
33 department of state revenue not later than sixty (60) days before the
34 date the increase takes effect that includes:
35 (1) the effective date for the increase; and
36 (2) the amount of the charge as increased by the board.
