DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 25-1-22-13.
Synopsis: Physician standards of practice. Specifies that the
provisions prohibiting a physician or practitioner from aiding or
abetting in the provision of gender transition procedures to a minor do
not prohibit a physician or other practitioner from: (1) releasing a
minor's health information to another physician or other practitioner in
the course of the receiving practitioner's care of the minor; or (2)
discussing a minor's medical history with another physician or other
practitioner providing care to the minor.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Campbell, Errington, Pierce M,
Clere
January 6, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Public Health.

HOUSE BILL No. 1338
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning
professions and occupations.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 25-1-22-13, AS ADDED BY P.L.10-2023,
2 SECTION 1, IS AMENDED TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE
3 JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 13. (a) Except as provided in subsections (c) and
4 (d), a physician or other practitioner may not knowingly provide gender
5 transition procedures to a minor.
6 (b) Except as provided in subsection (c), a physician or other
7 practitioner may not aid or abet another physician or practitioner in the
8 provision of gender transition procedures to a minor. This section does
9 not prohibit a physician or other practitioner from:
10 (1) releasing a minor's health information to another
11 physician or other practitioner in the course of the receiving
12 practitioner's care of the minor; or
13 (2) discussing a minor's medical history with another
14 physician or other practitioner providing care to the minor.
15 (c) This section does not prohibit a physician or other practitioner
16 from providing any of the following to a minor:
17 (1) Services to individuals born with a medically verifiable

1 disorder of sex development, including an individual with

4 chromosomes with virilization, born with forty-six (46) XY

7 (2) Services provided when a physician or practitioner has
8 diagnosed a disorder of sexual development that the physician or

10 testing that the individual does not have normal sex chromosome

13 (3) The treatment of any infection, injury, disease, or disorder that

16 (4) Any medical or surgical service undertaken because the

18 physical illness that would, as certified by a physician or
19 practitioner, place the individual in imminent danger of death or
20 impairment of major bodily function unless the medical or
21 surgical service is performed.
22 (d) A physician or practitioner within the practitioner's scope of
23 practice may continue to prescribe to an individual, who was taking a
24 gender transition hormone therapy on June 30, 2023, as part of a
25 gender transition procedure, gender transition hormone therapy until
26 December 31, 2023. This subsection expires January 1, 2024.
