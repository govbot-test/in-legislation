DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 27-1-34-11.
Synopsis: MEWA for child care facilities. Allows a group or
association of child care facilities to establish a multiple employer
welfare arrangement (MEWA). Requires the department of insurance
to: (1) create a survey requesting certain information from child care
facilities; (2) facilitate communication between child care facilities that
express an interest in establishing a MEWA; and (3) advise the child
care facilities on best practices for lowering insurance costs. Requires
the office of the secretary of family and social services to send a direct
link to the survey by electronic mail to each child care facility.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Dant Chesser, Clere, Hamilton
January 8, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Insurance.

HOUSE BILL No. 1400
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning
insurance.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 27-1-34-11 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA CODE
2 AS A NEW SECTION TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE JULY
3 1, 2026]: Sec. 11. (a) As used in this section, "child care facility"
4 means any of the following:
5 (1) A child care center (as defined in IC 12-7-2-28.4).
6 (2) A child care home (as defined in IC 12-7-2-28.6).
7 (b) A group or association of child care facilities may establish
8 a multiple employer welfare arrangement.
9 (c) Not later than September 1, 2026, the department shall
10 create a survey requesting the following information from child
11 care facilities:
12 (1) The name of the child care facility that is responding to the
13 survey.
14 (2) Whether the child care facility is interested in establishing
15 a multiple employer welfare arrangement.
16 (3) The child care facility's current insurance costs for
17 accident and sickness and death benefits.

1 The department must publish the survey on the department's
2 website.
3 (d) The department shall notify the office of the secretary of
4 family and social services when the survey is published on the
5 department's website. Not later than fourteen (14) calendar days
6 after receiving notice from the department under this subsection,
7 the office of the secretary of family and social services shall send a
8 direct link to the survey by electronic mail to each child care
9 facility.
10 (e) The department shall:
11 (1) facilitate communication between child care facilities that
12 express an interest in establishing a multiple employer welfare
