DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 35-43-4-2.8.
Synopsis: Key fob emulators. Provides (with certain exceptions) that
a person who knowingly or intentionally manufactures, sells, offers to
sell, possesses, operates, or uses a motor vehicle key programming
device commits unlawful use of a motor vehicle key programming
device, a Class A misdemeanor.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Crider
January 8, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Corrections and Criminal
Law.

SENATE BILL No. 207
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning
criminal law and procedure.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 35-43-4-2.8 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA CODE
2 AS A NEW SECTION TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE JULY
3 1, 2026]: Sec. 2.8. (a) This section does not apply to the following:
4 (1) A law enforcement officer acting within the scope of the
5 officer's duties.
6 (2) An employee of a:
7 (A) new motor vehicle dealer (as defined in
8 IC 9-32-2.1-32);
9 (B) used motor vehicle dealer (as defined in
10 IC 9-32-2.1-44);
11 (C) distributor (as defined in IC 9-13-2-45);
12 (D) manufacturer (as defined in IC 9-13-2-97);
13 (E) recreational vehicle dealer (as defined in
14 IC 9-32-19-13);
15 (F) recreational vehicle distributor (as defined in
16 IC 9-32-19-15);
17 (G) recreational vehicle manufacturer (as defined in
2026 IN 207—LS 6741/DI 151
2
1 IC 9-32-19-16);
2 (H) locksmith; or
3 (I) manufacturer of a motor vehicle key programming
4 device that is under contract for sale only to entities listed
5 in clauses (A) through (H);
6 acting within the scope of the employee's duties.
7 (b) As used in this section, "motor vehicle key programming
8 device" means a computer program, a device, an application, or a
9 software program designed to:
10 (1) intercept communications of; or
11 (2) electronically communicate with;
12 a motor vehicle for the purpose of emulating a motor vehicle key.
13 The term includes an emulating device and a relay attack device.
14 (c) A person may not do any of the following:
15 (1) Manufacture, offer for sale, market, transfer, or sell a
16 motor vehicle key programming device.
17 (2) Possess, use, or operate a motor vehicle key programming
18 device.
19 (d) A person who knowingly or intentionally violates subsection
20 (c) commits unlawful use of a motor vehicle key programming
21 device, a Class A misdemeanor.
