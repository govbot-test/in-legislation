DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 5-2-27.4.
Synopsis: Advanced DNA testing for cold cases. Establishes a process
concerning eligibility for advanced DNA testing for cold cases.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Hamilton
January 5, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Courts and Criminal Code.

HOUSE BILL No. 1248
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning state
and local administration.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 5-2-27.4 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA CODE
2 AS A NEW CHAPTER TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE
3 JULY 1, 2026]:
4 Chapter 27.4. Advanced DNA Testing for Cold Cases
5 Sec. 1. This chapter applies to law enforcement investigations or
6 county coroner investigations concerning the following:
7 (1) Homicide (IC 35-42-1).
8 (2) A sex offense (as defined by IC 11-8-8-5.2).
9 (3) High risk missing persons (as defined in IC 5-2-17-1).
10 Sec. 2. As used in this chapter, "advanced DNA testing"
11 includes:
12 (1) DNA forensics;
13 (2) DNA analytics; or
14 (3) genetic genealogy.
15 Sec. 3. As used in this chapter, "cold case" means either:
16 (1) a law enforcement investigation that:
17 (A) has been open for more than five (5) years; and

2
1 (B) has no pending charges; or
2 (2) a county coroner investigation concerning the
3 identification of unknown victims.
4 Sec. 4. As used in this chapter, "DNA profile" has the meaning
5 set forth in IC 10-13-6-4.
6 Sec. 5. As used in this chapter, "eligible family member" means:
7 (1) an individual related to the victim by blood, half-blood,
8 adoption, marriage, or remarriage, including:
9 (A) a spouse;
10 (B) a parent or stepparent;
11 (C) a child or stepchild;
12 (D) a grandchild or stepgrandchild;
13 (E) a grandparent or stepgrandparent;
14 (F) a brother, sister, stepbrother, or stepsister;
15 (G) a niece or nephew;
16 (H) an aunt or uncle;
17 (I) a daughter-in-law or son-in-law;
18 (J) a mother-in-law or father-in-law; or
19 (K) a first cousin; or
20 (2) an individual who cohabits or formerly cohabited with the
21 victim.
22 Sec. 6. As used in this chapter, "law enforcement agency" has
23 the meaning set forth in IC 35-47-15-2.
24 Sec. 7. As used in this chapter, "testing laboratory" means a
25 laboratory that:
26 (1) conducts advanced DNA testing; and
27 (2) adheres to the standards described in IC 10-13-6-14.
28 Sec. 8. As used in this chapter, "victim" means:
29 (1) a person who has suffered direct harm as a result of a
30 crime; or
31 (2) a high risk missing person (as defined in IC 5-2-17-1).
32 Sec. 9. The following individuals may submit a request to Crime
33 Stoppers of Central Indiana to determine eligibility for advanced
34 DNA testing:
35 (1) The victim of a cold case.
36 (2) An eligible family member of a cold case victim.
37 (3) A county coroner.
38 Sec. 10. Crime Stoppers of Central Indiana shall determine
39 eligibility for advanced DNA testing based on the following:
40 (1) The order in which the request is received.
41 (2) The date of the crime.
42 (3) The availability of funds.

3
1 (4) Any other relevant criteria as determined by Crime
2 Stoppers of Central Indiana.
3 Sec. 11. Upon a determination of eligibility under section 10 of
4 this chapter, Crime Stoppers of Central Indiana shall notify the
5 investigating law enforcement agency of the determination of
6 eligibility.
7 Sec. 12. Upon notice of eligibility under section 11 of this
8 chapter, the investigating law enforcement agency and a testing
9 laboratory shall collaborate to determine:
10 (1) the amount of DNA evidence available for testing; and
11 (2) the most appropriate advanced DNA testing options.
12 Sec. 13. A testing laboratory that conducts advanced DNA
13 testing shall collect, preserve, and maintain the appropriate chain
14 of custody of any evidence.
15 Sec. 14. The investigating law enforcement agency shall
16 transmit any information concerning a DNA profile to the state
17 police department for inclusion in the Indiana DNA data base and
18 the combined DNA index system (as defined in IC 10-13-6-1).
19 Sec. 15. Nothing in this chapter shall prohibit a law enforcement
20 agency from transferring DNA evidence to a testing laboratory for
21 advanced DNA testing at the law enforcement agency's own
22 expense.
