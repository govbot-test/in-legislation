MR. PRESIDENT:
I offer the following resolution and move its adoption:
1 A SENATE RESOLUTION recognizing the sister ties
2 between Indiana and Taiwan.
3 Whereas, In November 1979, the State of Indiana became
4 Taiwan's first sister state after signing a sister-state
5 relationship agreement with Taiwan, officially called the
6 Republic of China (R.O.C.), and ever since, Indiana and
7 Taiwan have enjoyed strong bilateral trade, educational and
8 cultural exchanges, and tourism;
9 Whereas, The year 2026 marks both the 250th anniversary
10 of the founding of the United States (U.S.) as well as the 30th
11 anniversary of Taiwan’s first direct presidential election;
12 Whereas, Taiwan shares the same values of freedom,
13 democracy, human rights, the rule of law, peace, and prosperity
14 with the U.S. and the State of Indiana;
15 Whereas, In 2022, Indiana Governor Eric Holcomb led a
16 delegation to Taiwan and signed a memorandum of
17 understanding on Indiana-Taiwan economic cooperation and
18 trade relations with Taiwan, becoming the first Indiana
19 governor to visit Taiwan since Governor Mitch Daniels in

2 Whereas, The economic cooperation and trade relations
3 have continued, with the Indiana Economic Development
4 Corporation opening an office in Taipei, Taiwan, in August
5 2024, and Governor Mike Braun witnessing the new expansion
6 of the research and development center from Taiwan’s
7 MediaTek, Inc., one of the world's largest semiconductor design
8 companies, in West Lafayette in December 2025;
9 Whereas, The U.S. ranks as Taiwan's second-largest
10 trading partner and Taiwan is the seventh-largest trading
11 partner of the U.S., ranking higher than the United Kingdom,
12 India, and Australia;
13 Whereas, Investments of Taiwan-affiliated firms in the U.S.
14 have nearly doubled since 2019, reaching $99.5 billion, and the
15 trade and investment with Taiwan helped support 387,000
16 well-paid American jobs in the U.S.;
17 Whereas, Bilateral trade reached more than $185 billion in
18 2024, and Taiwan is the twelfth-largest export market for U.S.
19 goods;
20 Whereas, Taiwan and the State of Indiana have enjoyed a

23 export market in Asia, with $262 million worth of Indiana
24 goods exported to Taiwan and a $16 million investment from
25 Taiwan to the State of Indiana;
26 Whereas, Taiwan sent its eighth agricultural goodwill trade
