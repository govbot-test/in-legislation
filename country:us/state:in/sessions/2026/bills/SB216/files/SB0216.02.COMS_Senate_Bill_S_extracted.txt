DIGEST OF SB 216 (Updated January 15, 2026 12:34 pm - DI 87)
Citations Affected: IC 32-24; IC 36-1.
Synopsis: Eminent domain. Provides that if a unit of local government
(unit) provides forgivable loans, the unit must (as a condition of
providing forgivable loans) require that a person operating a business
on a property may be entitled to compensation for business losses
resulting from a condemnation of the property.
Effective: Upon passage.
Buck, Niemeyer
January 8, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Local Government.
January 15, 2026, amended, reported favorably — Do Pass; reassigned to Committee on
Appropriations.

20 Chapter 2.6. Compensation for Business Losses
21 Sec. 1. This chapter applies only to condemnation proceedings
22 filed after March 31, 2026. This chapter only applies to a unit
23 under IC 36-1-8-20.5.
24 Sec. 2. As used in this chapter, "going concern" means the
25 benefits that accrue to a business or trade as a result of the
26 business's or trade's:
27 (1) location;
28 (2) geographic features;
29 (3) reputation for dependability;
30 (4) skill or quality;
31 (5) customer base; or
32 (6) good will;
33 or any other circumstances resulting in the probable retention of
34 old or acquisition of new patronage.
35 Sec. 3. As used in this chapter, "owner" means:
36 (1) the persons listed on the tax assessment rolls as being
37 responsible for the payment of real estate taxes imposed on
38 the property;
39 (2) the persons in whose name title to real estate is shown in
40 the records of the recorder of the county in which the real
41 estate is located; or
42 (3) a lessee who operates a business on the real property.
SB 216—LS 6928/DI 129
6
1 Sec. 4. If a business or trade is damaged by a taking,
2 condemnation, or eminent domain proceeding, the owner shall be
3 compensated by the condemnor for the loss of the going concern,
4 unless the condemnor establishes by a preponderance of the
5 evidence that:
6 (1) the loss is not caused by the taking of the property or the
7 injury to the remainder;
8 (2) the loss can be reasonably prevented by:
9 (A) relocating the business or trade to:
10 (i) the same or a similar location; and
11 (ii) a reasonably suitable location;
12 as the property that was taken; or
13 (B) taking steps and adopting procedures that a reasonably
14 prudent person:
15 (i) of a similar age; and
16 (ii) as the owner, under similar conditions;
17 would take and adopt in preserving the going concern of
18 the business or trade; or
19 (3) compensation for the loss will be duplicated in the
20 compensation otherwise awarded to the owner.
21 Sec. 5. An owner's damages for loss of value to a going concern
22 shall be determined by appraisers as part of the compensation due
23 to the owner. An owner shall provide an appraiser reasonable
24 access to the records necessary to determine the value of the loss of
25 the going concern. The appraiser's decision regarding any award
26 for the loss of the going concern may be challenged by any party.
27 SECTION 8. IC 36-1-8-20.5 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA CODE
28 AS A NEW SECTION TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE
29 UPON PASSAGE]: Sec. 20.5. (a) This section applies after March
30 31, 2026.
31 (b) If a unit provides a forgivable loan to a person, the unit must
32 require, as a condition of providing a forgivable loan, that an
33 owner (as defined in IC 32-24-2.6-3) of a business may be entitled
34 to compensation for loss of a going concern under IC 32-24-2.6.
