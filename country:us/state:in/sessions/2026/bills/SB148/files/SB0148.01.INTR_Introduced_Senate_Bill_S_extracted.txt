DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 36-8-25.5.
Synopsis: Indiana crime guns task force. Adds LaPorte County and
Porter County to the task force area of the Indiana crime guns task
force (task force). Increases the number of members of the executive
board of the task force required for a quorum and to take official action
in the event of a tie vote.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Pol Jr., Freeman
January 5, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Corrections and Criminal
Law.

SENATE BILL No. 148
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning local
government.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 36-8-25.5-1, AS AMENDED BY P.L.16-2025,
2 SECTION 1, IS AMENDED TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE
3 JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 1. The following definitions apply throughout this
4 chapter:
5 (1) "Executive board" means the task force executive board
6 established by section 3 of this chapter.
7 (2) "Task force" means the Indiana crime guns task force
8 established by section 2 of this chapter.
9 (3) "Task force area" means one (1) or more of the following
10 counties:
11 (A) Boone County.
12 (B) Hamilton County.
13 (C) Hancock County.
14 (D) Hendricks County.
15 (E) Marion County.
16 (F) Morgan County.
17 (G) Johnson County.
2026 IN 148—LS 6553/DI 87
2
1 (H) Shelby County.
2 (I) Allen County.
3 (J) Lake County.
4 (K) LaPorte County.
5 (L) Porter County.
6 SECTION 2. IC 36-8-25.5-3, AS AMENDED BY P.L.16-2025,
7 SECTION 2, IS AMENDED TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE
8 JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 3. (a) The task force executive board is
9 established to oversee and direct the operations of the task force.
10 (b) The executive board consists of:
11 (1) the chief of police of the Indianapolis metropolitan police
12 department or the chief's designee, who serves as the chairperson
13 of the executive board;
14 (2) the superintendent of the Indiana state police department or
15 the superintendent's designee, who serves as the vice chairperson
16 of the executive board; and
17 (3) a sheriff or chief of police from each department in the task
18 force area which has assigned an officer to the task force.
19 (c) The executive board shall hold meetings at the call of the
20 chairperson. The executive board may establish rules governing
21 meetings.
22 (d) Seven (7) Eight (8) executive board members constitute a
23 quorum for the transaction of business. Each member has one (1) vote,
24 and action by the executive board may be taken only upon the
25 affirmative votes of the majority of attending members. If a vote is a
26 tie, the position for which the chairperson voted prevails, as long as
27 that position has received the affirmative votes of at least four (4) five
28 (5) members.
29 (e) A member of the executive board is not entitled to:
30 (1) the minimum salary per diem provided by IC 4-10-11-2.1(b);
31 or
32 (2) reimbursement for traveling and other expenses as provided
33 under IC 4-13-1-4.
