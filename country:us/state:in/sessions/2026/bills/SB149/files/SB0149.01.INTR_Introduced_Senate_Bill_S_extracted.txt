DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 9-21-3.7; IC 35-52-9.1-12.8.
Synopsis: Tolling transponders. Allows a person who: (1) lives or
works in a county where there is a toll road or tollway; and (2) pays for
tolls assessed while driving on the toll road or tollway with a
transponder; to apply for a credit to be added to the person's qualifying
account for use towards future tolls. Provides that a person who pays
for tolls using a transponder that is connected to a qualified account
that is not registered to: (1) the person; (2) the person's employer; (3)
a car rental company that owns the car being driven by the person; or
(4) another person with the same permanent residence as the person;
commits a Class B misdemeanor.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Pol Jr.
January 5, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Homeland Security and
Transportation.

SENATE BILL No. 149
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning
motor vehicles.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 9-21-3.7 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA CODE
2 AS A NEW CHAPTER TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE
3 JULY 1, 2026]:
4 Chapter 3.7. Tolling Transponders
5 Sec. 1. As used in this chapter, "qualifying account" means an
6 active electronic toll collection customer account associated with
7 a certain transponder.
8 Sec. 2. As used in this chapter, "toll road" has the meaning set
9 forth in IC 9-21-3.5-7.
10 Sec. 3. As used in this chapter, "tollway" has the meaning set
11 forth in IC 9-21-3.5-8.
12 Sec. 4. As used in this chapter, "transponder" means a device,
13 placed on or within a motor vehicle, that is capable of transmitting
14 information used to assess or collect tolls.
15 Sec. 5. (a) A person with a qualifying account that:
16 (1) has a permanent residence or a place of employment
17 located in a county with a toll road or tollway; and

1 (2) pays for tolls assessed while traveling on the toll road or
2 tollway described in subdivision (1) in the person's personal
3 vehicle through the person's transponder;
4 may apply for a future credit on the person's transponder in an
5 amount equal to seventy-five percent (75%) of the amount paid for
6 tolls since the person's last application for a future credit under
7 this subsection.
8 (b) A person may apply for a future credit under subsection (a)
9 not more than once every thirty (30) days.
10 (c) Not later than December 31, 2026, the bureau shall provide
11 a method for applying to the bureau for a future credit under
12 subsection (a) on the bureau's website. The bureau may require a
13 person to provide proof of residency or proof of employment in a
14 county with a toll road or a tollway on a periodic basis.
15 (d) The bureau must provide the person administering the
16 qualified account with the amount of the credit not later than ten
17 (10) days after an application is approved. The person
18 administering the qualified account must load the credit on to the
19 qualified account not later than ten (10) days after the bureau
20 provides the person with the amount of the credit.
21 (e) The state comptroller shall make payments to an operator
22 (as defined in IC 9-21-3.5-4) from the state general fund at least
23 once every ninety (90) days in the amount of those credits used by
24 qualified accounts to pay for tolls that would have been paid to the
25 operator.
26 Sec. 6. A person who uses a transponder that is not registered
27 under a qualifying account owned by:
28 (1) the person;
29 (2) the person's employer;
30 (3) a car rental company that owns the car being driven by
31 the person; or
32 (4) another person with the same permanent residence as the
33 person;
34 to pay for a toll or user fee while traveling on a toll road or tollway
35 commits a Class B misdemeanor.
36 Sec. 7. All information collected by a transponder and regarding
37 a qualifying account is confidential and not subject to disclosure
38 under IC 5-14-3.
