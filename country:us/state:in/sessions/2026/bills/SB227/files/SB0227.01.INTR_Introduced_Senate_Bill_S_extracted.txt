DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 10-22; IC 34-30-2.1-127.5.
Synopsis: Counter action against unmanned aircraft systems (UAS).
Provides that the state police department is the statewide coordinating
agency for counter-UAS activities authorized under federal law.
Provides that the bill's provisions apply: (1) if a federal law is enacted
that authorizes state or local law enforcement personnel to detect, track,
identify, or mitigate a UAS under federal approval, certification, or
oversight; and (2) after the governor publishes a notice in the Indiana
Register that includes a description of the authorization and identifies
the federal statute or program that provides the authorization. Provides
that the state police department may designate a law enforcement
agency of a political subdivision as a participating agency if the agency
satisfies federal requirements for personnel training and operational
readiness. Provides that the state police department may designate
Indianapolis as a pilot project location due to the concentration of
high-risk sites and special event assessment rating events. Provides that
mitigation activities may not be performed unless expressly authorized
under federal law.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Baldwin, Freeman, Carrasco
January 8, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Homeland Security and
Transportation.

A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning
public safety.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 10-22 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA CODE AS
2 A NEW ARTICLE TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE JULY 1,
3 2026]:
4 ARTICLE 22. COUNTER-UNMANNED AIRCRAFT SYSTEM
5 AUTHORITY
6 Chapter 1. Applicability
7 Sec. 1. This article applies:
8 (1) if a federal law is enacted that authorizes state or local law
9 enforcement personnel to detect, track, identify, or mitigate
10 an unmanned aircraft system under federal approval,
11 certification, or oversight; and
12 (2) after the governor publishes a notice in the Indiana
13 Register that includes a description of this section and
14 identifies the federal statute or program that provides the
15 authorization described in subdivision (1).
16 Chapter 2. Definitions
17 Sec. 1. The following definitions apply throughout this article:
2026 IN 227—LS 6954/DI 116
2
1 (1) "Counter-UAS system" means an unmanned aircraft
2 detection or mitigation system approved under federal law
3 and listed on the federal C-UAS Technology List jointly
4 maintained by the:
5 (A) United States Department of Justice;
6 (B) United States Department of Homeland Security;
7 (C) United States Department of War;
8 (D) United States Department of Transportation;
9 (E) Federal Communications Commission; and
10 (F) National Telecommunications and Information
11 Administration.
12 (2) "High-risk site" includes a large scale public event, a
13 federally rated special event assessment rating event, critical
14 infrastructure, a correctional facility, an airport, or any other
15 location designated by the state police department consistent
16 with federal requirements.
17 (3) "Mitigation" means an action authorized by federal law to
18 disrupt, disable, seize control of, or otherwise neutralize an
19 unmanned aircraft system.
20 (4) "Participating agency" means a law enforcement agency
21 designated under this article to perform counter-UAS
22 functions in accordance with federal authorization.
23 (5) "Trained personnel" means individuals who have
24 completed training and certification at the national
25 counter-UAS schoolhouse maintained by the United States
26 Department of Justice and the United States Department of
27 Homeland Security, as required under federal law.
28 (6) "Unmanned aircraft system" or "UAS" has the meaning
29 set forth in 49 U.S.C. 44801.
30 Chapter 3. Statewide Coordination
31 Sec. 1. The state police department is the statewide coordinating
32 agency for counter-UAS activities authorized under federal law.
33 Sec. 2. Subject to IC 10-22-1, the state police department shall:
34 (1) apply to the appropriate federal agencies for authorization
35 to acquire, deploy, operate, and train with counter-UAS
36 systems;
37 (2) enter into agreements required by federal law or federal
38 policy;
39 (3) ensure all acquired systems are on the federal C-UAS
40 Technology List;
41 (4) administer a statewide program governing operational
42 procedures, reporting, training, and compliance;

3
1 (5) oversee federal audits and compliance reviews; and
2 (6) maintain records of counter-UAS activities required by
3 federal agencies.
4 Sec. 3. The state police department may adopt rules under
5 IC 4-22-2 to implement this article.
6 Chapter 4. Participating Agencies and Pilot Project Location
7 Sec. 1. The state police department may designate a law
8 enforcement agency of a political subdivision as a participating
9 agency if the agency satisfies federal requirements for personnel
10 training and operational readiness.
11 Sec. 2. The state police department may designate the city of
12 Indianapolis as a pilot project location due to the concentration of
13 high-risk sites and special event assessment rating events. A
14 location with a pilot project designation must comply with federal
15 requirements.
16 Chapter 5. Authorized Activities
17 Sec. 1. Trained personnel within the scope of federal
18 authorization may perform detection, identification, tracking,
19 warning, disruption, control seizure, confiscation, or disabling of
20 an unmanned aircraft system only to the extent authorized by
21 federal law and subject to federal approval.
22 Sec. 2. Mitigation activities may not be performed under this
23 article unless expressly authorized under federal law.
24 Chapter 6. Reporting, Deconfliction, and Revocation
25 Sec. 1. The state police department shall participate in any
26 federal drone reporting or deconfliction system applicable to state
27 or local law enforcement agencies.
28 Sec. 2. A participating agency shall submit to the state police
29 department a post-event report within the time required under
30 federal law for notification to federal agencies.
31 Sec. 3. The superintendent of the state police department shall
32 suspend or revoke participation if required by federal audit, if a
33 participating agency fails to comply with federal or state
34 procedures or if federal authorization is withdrawn.
35 Chapter 7. Procurement and Funding
36 Sec. 1. The state police department and participating agencies
37 may receive federal grants for acquiring counter-UAS systems
38 listed on the federal approved list, for training at the national
39 counter-UAS schoolhouse, and for administrative costs.
40 Sec. 2. A counter-UAS system may not be purchased or
41 deployed unless the system appears on the federal C-UAS
42 Technology List.

1 Chapter 8. Liability
2 Sec. 1. Activities performed under this article by trained
3 personnel within the scope of federal authorization are actions
4 within the scope of employment for purposes of IC 34-13-3.
5 Chapter 9. Construction
6 Sec. 1. This article shall be interpreted to conform to federal
7 law. If a conflict exists between this article and a federal
8 requirement applicable to counter-UAS activities, the federal
9 requirement controls.
