HOUSE BILL No. 1167
_____
DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 10-19-14.
Synopsis: Digital alert technology pilot program. Establishes the
digital alert pilot program (pilot program) administered by the
department of homeland security (department) to provide grants to
providers of fire, emergency medical, and law enforcement services for
the use of digital alert technology to notify motorists regarding the
location of providers' emergency vehicles. Establishes the digital alert
pilot program grant fund. Requires the secretary of public safety and
the executive director of the Indiana criminal justice institute to
provide a plan to the department not later than September 1, 2026, for
leveraging available federal funds to fund the pilot program.
Effective: July 1, 2026.

4 Chapter 14. Digital Alert Pilot Program
5 Sec. 1. As used in this chapter, "applicant" means any of the

11 (5) A volunteer fire department.

13 means an authorized emergency vehicle (as defined in IC 9-13-2-6)
14 of an applicant.
15 Sec. 3. As used in this chapter, "digital alert technology" means
16 technology that is capable of automatically sending a digital alert:
17 (1) when an emergency vehicle's lights are activated; and

16 Sec. 8. The digital alert pilot program is established to provide
17 grants to eligible applicants for the use of digital alert technology
18 to reduce motorist collisions with authorized emergency vehicles
19 by providing notice to a motorist of the location of an authorized
20 emergency vehicle that is:
21 (1) en route to an emergency; or
22 (2) parked at an emergency scene, including in or alongside a
23 highway, street, or road.
24 Sec. 9. (a) The pilot program shall be administered by the
25 department.
26 (b) The department shall develop criteria regarding the
27 expenditures for which a grant may be awarded, including
28 expenditures for:
29 (1) the purchase or rental of dedicated transponders for
30 installation and use in authorized emergency vehicles; and
31 (2) the purchase of subscriptions to digital alerting platforms
32 and cloud storage.
33 Sec. 10. The department shall do the following:
34 (1) Prescribe the form and manner in which grant
35 applications may be submitted.
36 (2) Determine the eligibility of applicants and the selection
37 procedures. The department must require as a condition of
38 eligibility that an applicant must complete annual defensive
39 driving training courses.
40 (3) Determine whether the equipment or service for which an
41 applicant seeks a grant fulfills the purposes of this chapter.
42 (4) Determine the amount of a grant awarded to a recipient.
