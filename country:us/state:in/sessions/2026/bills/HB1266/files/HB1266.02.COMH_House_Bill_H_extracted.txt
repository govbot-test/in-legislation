Synopsis: Department of education and education matters. Makes
various changes to education provisions concerning the following: (1)
Schools eligible for grants from the Indiana secured school fund,
teacher appreciation grants, grants under the robotics competition
program, and funding for certain advanced placement examinations. (2)
Creating and updating a list of employers that have agreed to provide
career support for or interview Indiana residents who graduate with an
Indiana diploma with an employment honors plus seal. (3) Creating a
teaching and learning framework for the implementation of
mathematics academic standards. (4) Development of a data science
math pathway. (5) Establishing a process and strategies to support
academic and fiscally underperforming schools, development of a plan
to intervene, and submission of the process and plan to the general
assembly. (6) Evaluating, approving, and publishing high quality
curricular materials time lines. (7) Local board membership for a local
centralized school facilities board and local student transportation
board. (8) Salary differentiation regarding teachers who possess a
literacy endorsement. (9) Amounts expended in participating in an
interlocal cooperative included in determining amounts school
corporations expend on full-time teacher salaries for purposes of
teacher salary minimum funding requirements. (10) Summer school
program and reimbursement administration. (11) Criteria required for
eligibility of a waiver for conducting four day school weeks. (12)
Oversight of career and technical education program designation. (13)

3 Sec. 2. Article I, purpose. The purpose of this compact is to
4 facilitate the mobility of teachers across the member states, with
5 the goal of supporting teachers through a new pathway to
6 licensure. Through this compact, the member states seek to
7 establish a collective regulatory framework that expedites and
8 enhances the ability of teachers to move across state lines. This
9 compact is intended to achieve the following objectives and should
10 be interpreted accordingly. The member states hereby ratify the
11 same intentions by subscribing to:
12 (1) Create a streamlined pathway to licensure mobility for
13 teachers.
14 (2) Support the relocation of eligible military spouses.
15 (3) Facilitate and enhance the exchange of licensure,
16 investigative, and disciplinary information between member
17 states.
18 (4) Enhance the power of state and district level education
19 officials to hire qualified, competent teachers by removing
20 barriers to the employment of out-of-state teachers.
21 (5) Support the retention of teachers in the profession by
22 removing barriers to relicensure in a new state.
23 (6) Maintain state sovereignty in the regulation of the
24 teaching profession.
