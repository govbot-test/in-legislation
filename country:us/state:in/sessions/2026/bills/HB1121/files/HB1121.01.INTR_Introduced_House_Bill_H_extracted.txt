DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 5-2-27.1.
Synopsis: Drug and alcohol testing. Provides that a law enforcement
agency that employs a law enforcement officer involved in an
officer-involved shooting must administer alcohol and drug testing to
the applicable law enforcement officer.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Bartlett
January 5, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Veterans Affairs and Public
Safety.

HOUSE BILL No. 1121
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning state
and local administration.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 5-2-27.1 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA CODE
2 AS A NEW CHAPTER TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE
3 JULY 1, 2026]:
4 Chapter 27.1. Law Enforcement Officer Drug and Alcohol
5 Testing After Officer-Involved Shooting
6 Sec. 1. As used in this chapter, "law enforcement agency" has
7 the meaning set forth in IC 5-2-17-2.
8 Sec. 2. As used in this chapter, "law enforcement officer" has
9 the meaning set forth in IC 5-2-1-2.
10 Sec. 3. As used in this chapter, "officer-involved shooting"
11 means any instance when a law enforcement officer discharges
12 their firearm, causing injury or death to a person, in the line of
13 duty.
14 Sec. 4. (a) A law enforcement agency that employs a law
15 enforcement officer involved in an officer-involved shooting must
16 administer alcohol and drug testing to the applicable law
17 enforcement officer immediately after the officer-involved shooting
2026 IN 1121—LS 6499/DI 116
2
1 in accordance with protocols adopted by the law enforcement
2 agency described in subsection (b).
3 (b) Each law enforcement agency shall adopt and implement
4 protocols to administer subsection (a). The protocols shall
5 prescribe that the alcohol and drug testing must be administered
6 as soon as practicable after the officer-involved shooting but not
7 later than the end of the applicable law enforcement officer's shift
8 or work day.
