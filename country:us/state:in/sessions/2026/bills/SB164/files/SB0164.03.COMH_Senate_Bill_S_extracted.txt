DIGEST OF SB 164 (Updated February 19, 2026 8:55 am - DI 140)
Citations Affected: IC 15-11.
Synopsis: Study of community or urban microfarming. Requires the
department of agriculture (department) to conduct a study to evaluate
the feasibility of establishing community or urban microfarm zones
statewide. Requires the department to submit a report to the general
assembly containing certain findings and recommendations not later
than December 1, 2027.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Spencer, Tomes, Leising, Glick,
Yoder, Clark, Ford J.D.,
Randolph Lonnie M
(HOUSE SPONSORS — OLTHOFF, HARRIS, CASH, BARTLETT)

January 29, 2026, read third time, passed. Yeas 44, nays 4.

Development.

SENATE BILL No. 164
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning
agriculture and animals.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 15-11-2-10 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA CODE
2 AS A NEW SECTION TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE JULY
3 1, 2026]: Sec. 10. (a) The department shall conduct a study to
4 evaluate the feasibility of establishing community or urban
5 microfarm zones statewide.
6 (b) The study conducted under subsection (a) must include the
7 following:
8 (1) A summary of existing studies and literature regarding the
9 feasibility of establishing community or urban microfarm
10 zones statewide.
11 (2) Identification of zoning templates and best practices for
12 community or urban microfarm management.
13 (3) Opportunities for partnerships with the Purdue University
14 cooperative extension service, local health departments,
15 health care providers, and educational institutions.
16 (c) Not later than December 1, 2027, the department shall
17 submit a report to the general assembly, in an electronic format
ES 164—LS 6716/DI 148
2
1 under IC 5-14-6, with the following information:
2 (1) The findings regarding subsection (b)(1) through (b)(3).
3 (2) A specific finding as to:
4 (A) whether and how community or urban microfarm
5 zones could work in Indiana; and
6 (B) what options are available for future pilot programs or
7 technical assistance.
8 (3) A recommendation for:
9 (A) standards to ensure safety and compatibility; and
10 (B) future legislation to implement community or urban
11 microfarm zones.
