1 A HOUSE RESOLUTION recognizing February as
2 American Heart Month.
3 Whereas, Cardiac arrest is a leading cause of death
4 worldwide, and each year, more than 350,000 cardiac arrests
5 occur outside of hospitals in the United States, with
6 approximately 10 percent of people surviving;
7 Whereas, About 70 percent of out-of-hospital cardiac arrests
8 occur at home, meaning that people nearby are the first and
9 most critical link in the chain of survival;

2
1 Whereas, Immediate cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR)
2 can double or even triple a person's chance of survival, yet only
3 about 41 percent of people who experience cardiac arrest
4 receive immediate CPR from someone nearby, and fewer than
5 12 percent receive aid from an automated external defibrillator
6 (AED) before advanced help arrives;
7 Whereas, For adults and teens, Hands-Only CPR, which
8 includes calling 911 and pushing hard and fast in the center of
9 the chest, can double or triple the chance of survival, while
10 infants and children require CPR with rescue breaths to restore
11 oxygen and circulation;
12 Whereas, Each year, more than 23,000 children experience
13 cardiac arrest outside of the hospital, with almost 40 percent
14 related to sports and nearly 20 percent occurring among
15 infants, and cardiac arrest accounts for 10 to 15 percent of
16 sudden unexpected infant deaths;
17 Whereas, Studies show that women are less likely than men
18 to receive CPR before advanced help arrives due to fear of
19 causing harm, perceived legal risk, or discomfort about
20 physical contact, and these factors must be addressed through
21 education and awareness;
22 Whereas, The American Heart Association has set a bold
23 goal to double survival from out-of-hospital cardiac arrest by
24 2030 through its Nation of Lifesavers initiative, empowering
25 people everywhere to perform CPR and use AEDs confidently;
26 and
27 Whereas, Straightforward, community-based solutions, such
28 as CPR and AED education, public access to defibrillators, and
29 cardiac emergency response plans (CERPs) in schools,
30 workplaces, and public spaces, can save lives and strengthen
31 community readiness: Therefore,
32 Be it resolved by the House of Representatives of the
33 General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
34 SECTION 1. That the Indiana House of Representatives
