DIGEST OF SB 59 (Updated January 12, 2026 2:06 pm - DI 87)
Citations Affected: IC 36-4.
Synopsis: Notice of annexation outreach meetings. Requires a
municipality to mail notice of the date, time, and location of annexation
outreach meetings to: (1) the county executive of each county; (2) the
township executive of each township; and (3) any plan commission; in
which the annexation territory is located.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Niemeyer, Dernulc
December 8, 2025, read first time and referred to Committee on Local Government.
January 8, 2026, reported favorably — Do Pass.
January 12, 2026, read second time, amended, ordered engrossed.

SENATE BILL No. 59
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning local
government.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 36-4-3-1.7, AS AMENDED BY P.L.70-2022,
2 SECTION 1, IS AMENDED TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE
3 JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 1.7. (a) This section applies only to an annexation
4 ordinance adopted after June 30, 2015. This section does not apply to
5 an annexation under section 5.1 of this chapter.
6 (b) Not earlier than six (6) months before a municipality introduces
7 an annexation ordinance, the municipality shall conduct an outreach
8 program to inform citizens regarding the proposed annexation. For an
9 annexation under section 3 or 4 of this chapter, the outreach program
10 must conduct at least six (6) public information meetings regarding the
11 proposed annexation. For an annexation under section 5 or 5.2 of this
12 chapter, the outreach program must conduct at least three (3) public
13 information meetings regarding the proposed annexation. The public
14 information meetings must provide citizens with the following
15 information:
16 (1) Maps showing the proposed boundaries of the annexation
17 territory.
SB 59—LS 6170/DI 87
2
1 (2) Proposed plans for extension of capital and noncapital
2 services in the annexation territory, including proposed dates of
3 extension. In the case of an annexation under section 5.2 of this
4 chapter, a copy of the preliminary written fiscal plan.
5 (3) Expected fiscal impact on taxpayers in the annexation
6 territory, including any increase in taxes and fees.
7 (c) The municipality shall provide notice of the dates, times, and
8 locations of the outreach program meetings. The municipality shall do
9 all of the following:
10 (1) Publish the notice of the meetings under IC 5-3-1, including
11 the date, time, and location of the meetings, except that notice
12 must be published not later than thirty (30) days before the date
13 of each meeting.
14 (2) The municipality shall also Send notice under subsection (d)
15 to each owner of land within the annexation territory not later
16 than thirty (30) days before the date of the first meeting of the
17 outreach program.
18 (3) Send notice by first class mail, certified mail with return
19 receipt requested, or any other means of delivery that
20 includes a return receipt not later than thirty (30) days before
21 the date of the first meeting of the outreach program to:
22 (A) the county executive body of each county;
23 (B) the township executive of each township; and
24 (C) any plan commission;
25 in which the annexation territory is located. The notice must
26 provide the date, time, and location of the meetings to be
27 conducted under the outreach program.
28 (d) The notice to landowners shall be sent by first class mail,
29 certified mail with return receipt requested, or any other means of
30 delivery that includes a return receipt and must include the following
31 information:
32 (1) The notice must inform the landowner that the municipality is
33 proposing to annex territory that includes the landowner's
34 property.
35 (2) The municipality is conducting an outreach program for the
36 purpose of providing information to landowners and the public
37 regarding the proposed annexation.
38 (3) The date, time, and location of the meetings to be conducted
39 under the outreach program.
40 (d) (e) The notice shall be sent to the address of the landowner as
41 listed on the tax duplicate. If the municipality provides evidence that
42 the notice was sent:
SB 59—LS 6170/DI 87
3
1 (1) by certified mail, with return receipt requested or any other
2 means of delivery that includes a return receipt; and
3 (2) in accordance with this section;
4 it is not necessary that the landowner accept receipt of the notice. If a
5 remonstrance is filed under section 11 of this chapter, the municipality
6 shall file with the court proof that notices were sent to landowners
7 under this section and proof of publication.
8 (e) (f) The notice required under this section is in addition to any
9 notice required under sections 2.1 and 2.2 of this chapter.
