Section 1:
PRINTING CODE. Amendments: Whenever an existing statute (or a section of the Indiana
Constitution) is being amended, the text of the existing provision will appear in this style type,
additions will appear in this style type, and deletions will appear in this style type.
Additions: Whenever a new statutory provision is being enacted (or a new constitutional
provision adopted), the text of the new provision will appear in this style type. Also, the
word NEW will appear in that style type in the introductory clause of each SECTION that adds
a new provision to the Indiana Code or the Indiana Constitution.
Conflict reconciliation: Text in a statute in this style type or this style type reconciles conflicts
between statutes enacted by the 2025 Regular Session of the General Assembly.
HOUSE ENROLLED ACT No. 1325
AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning education.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:

Section 2:
SECTION 1. IC 20-19-3-41.8 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA
CODE AS A NEW SECTION TO READ AS FOLLOWS
[EFFECTIVE JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 41.8. (a) Not later than October
1, 2026, the department, in collaboration with the Indiana
department of health, the department of child services, and the
office of the secretary of family and social services, shall
collaborate and submit to the general assembly in an electronic
format under IC 5-14-16, a report that includes information and,
as applicable, recommendations on the following:
(1) Data on the number of students who are using excess cost
funds for residential placement and the cost each public
school, including charter schools, expends yearly on required
payments to access state funds.
(2) Data on the amount each public school, including charter
schools, is expending above state provided funding on
developmental preschool.
(3) Data on the number of special education students receiving
homebound instruction in each public school, including
charter schools, and the associated costs for each public
school, including charter schools.
(4) Data on the costs, including transportation and interpreter
fees, incurred by each public school, including charter
