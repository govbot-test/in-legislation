DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 20-19-3-41.8.
Synopsis: Behavioral intervention and special education. Requires the
department of education, in collaboration with the Indiana department
of health, the department of child services, and the office of the
secretary of family and social services to report and make
recommendations concerning behavioral intervention services for
children and special education.
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Pfaff, Clere
January 6, 2026, read first time and referred to Committee on Education.

HOUSE BILL No. 1325
A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning
education.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 20-19-3-41.8 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA
2 CODE AS A NEW SECTION TO READ AS FOLLOWS
3 [EFFECTIVE JULY 1, 2026]: Sec. 41.8. (a) Not later than October
4 1, 2026, the department, in collaboration with the Indiana
5 department of health, the department of child services, and the
6 office of the secretary of family and social services, shall
7 collaborate and submit to the general assembly in an electronic
8 format under IC 5-14-16, a report that includes information and,
9 as applicable, recommendations on the following:
10 (1) The current and projected need for behavioral
11 intervention services for children, including estimated costs
12 for families, schools, and the justice system.
13 (2) An analysis of the implications if children do not receive
14 timely and appropriate behavioral intervention services for
15 juveniles, including safety and monetary risks.
16 (3) Each agency's processes, procedures, and practices
17 concerning acute behavioral intervention services for
