DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL
Citations Affected: IC 16-42-19-18; IC 35-52-16-88.7.
Synopsis: Possession of a syringe. Repeals the crime of possession of
a syringe in the health code (possession of a syringe remains a crime as
possession of paraphernalia in the criminal code).
Effective: July 1, 2026.
Clark
December 11, 2025, read first time and referred to Committee on Corrections and Criminal
Law.

SENATE BILL No. 126
A BILL FOR AN ACT to repeal a provision of the Indiana Code
concerning criminal law and procedure.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:
1 SECTION 1. IC 16-42-19-18 IS REPEALED [EFFECTIVE JULY
2 1, 2026]. Sec. 18. (a) A person may not possess with intent to:
3 (1) violate this chapter; or
4 (2) commit an offense described in IC 35-48-4;
5 a hypodermic syringe or needle or an instrument adapted for the use of
6 a controlled substance or legend drug by injection in a human being.
7 (b) A person who violates subsection (a) commits a Level 6 felony.
8 SECTION 2. IC 35-52-16-88.7 IS REPEALED [EFFECTIVE JULY
9 1, 2026]. Sec. 88.7. IC 16-42-19-18 defines a crime concerning
10 injection devices.
