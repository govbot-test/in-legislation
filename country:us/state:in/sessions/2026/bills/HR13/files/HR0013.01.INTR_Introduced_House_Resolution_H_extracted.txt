2 anniversary of the United States of America and the Irish ties to
3 the historical event.

5 the 11-month siege of Boston when they withdrew from the city,
6 leaving control of Boston to American forces, including
7 General John Sullivan, who was named Officer of the Day by
8 George Washington;
9 Whereas, General John Sullivan, the son of Irish immigrants,
10 would go on to be the Governor of New Hampshire, and his

3 Whereas, The Declaration of Independence had eight Irish
4 American signatories, including three who were born in
5 Ireland. These eight men were Thomas McKean, who later
6 served as President of the Continental Congress, Charles
7 Carroll, James Smith, George Taylor, Matthew Thornton,
8 Edward Rutledge, Thomas Lynch Jr., and George Read;
9 Whereas, These eight men came from different communities
10 and upbringings, ranging from the sons of lawyers and
11 clergymen to the sons of farmers and indentured servants. Yet
12 they stood united in their efforts to create a republic in the
13 United States, which inspired generations in Ireland to seek
14 and ultimately secure an Irish republic. The spirit of
15 independence they embodied is at the heart of the republics they
16 crucially helped to found in both America and Ireland;
17 Whereas, Their brand of Irish defiance and Enlightenment
18 Republican ideas was used to create an alternative society for
19 free people;
20 Whereas, The first 200 copies of the Declaration of
21 Independence were printed by John Dunlap, who emigrated
22 from County Tyrone, and it first appeared internationally in the
23 Belfast Newsletter in Ireland;
24 Whereas, 250 years ago, Stephen Moylan, George
25 Washington's Irish American aide-de-camp, first committed the
26 phrase "United States of America" to paper; and
27 Whereas, It was reported to the British Parliament by Lord
28 Mountjoy that, "America was lost by Irish emigrants...I am
29 assured from the best authority, a major part of the American
30 Army was composed of Irish and that the Irish language was as
31 commonly spoken in the American ranks as English, I am also
32 informed it was their valor that determined the contest.":
